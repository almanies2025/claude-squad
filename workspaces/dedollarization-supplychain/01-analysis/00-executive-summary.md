# Executive Summary: Is Dedollarization Real?

**Verdict: YES — but it's supply-chain rerouting, not a switch being flipped.**

---

## Core Finding

Dedollarization is a **structural force in its early-to-mid phase**, not a rhetorical narrative. The evidence from capital flows, infrastructure deployment, and institutional behavior confirms a genuine shift in global monetary architecture. However, the dollar is not being replaced — it is being **bypassed** in specific corridors by a patchwork of bilateral arrangements, gold accumulation, and parallel infrastructure.

The most accurate framing: **a slow erosion toward a multipolar monetary system**, with discrete trigger points that could accelerate the transition.

---

## Key Findings by Track

### 1. Capital Flows: Structural, Not Cyclical

China has been a **net seller of US Treasuries for 9 consecutive months** (as of Jan 2026). Holdings fell from $1.3T peak to $638B end-2025 — the lowest since 2008. Chinese regulators are now _directly urging_ financial institutions to reduce Treasury exposure. This is policy, not market noise.

GCC states show a **dual-track pattern**: issuing record dollar debt ($55B in GCC bank securities in 2025) while simultaneously reducing official reserve allocation to dollar assets and accumulating gold.

EM central banks bought **record gold in 2022-2024** (>1,000 tonnes/year). 2025 saw ~630-860 tonnes — still historically elevated. A record **43% of central banks** now plan to increase gold reserves. Zero surveyed plan to reduce them.

Dollar share of global FX reserves: **56.9%** (Q3 2025), down from 72% peak. Direction is clear and accelerating for new accumulation, not just the stock.

**Confidence: HIGH**

---

### 2. Payment Infrastructure: Early but Real

**mBridge crossed $55B in cumulative transactions** (self-reported by mBridge Coalition). BIS reporting shows ~$1.1B in cumulative transactions (2024). These figures are not directly comparable — Coalition figure may include projected or multi-year pipeline values. Treat the BIS figure as the conservative operational baseline.

> **Source caveat**: The $55B figure appears in Coalition communications. BIS published ~$1.1B as of 2024. The discrepancy likely reflects different measurement methodologies. The red team uses BIS conservative figure ($1.1B) as the floor — functionally marginal against $30T+ annual Gulf-US trade.

**mBridge reached MVP mid-2024**. Participants: China, Hong Kong, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Thailand.

**CIPS** (China's RMB settlement system) has **1,467 indirect participants globally** as of January 2025. China-Russia trade is fully dedollarized via CIPS bilateral corridors.

**BRICS Pay**: Announced 2024, **not operational as a unified system**. BRICS has 11 members with fundamentally incompatible monetary interests (yuan dominance vs. rupee balance vs. dollar hedging). No common settlement layer exists.

**SWIFT**: Still handles ~80% of cross-border payments. But US weaponization of SWIFT (Russia 2022, Iran) has created permanent incentive for every non-aligned country to develop alternatives.

**Confidence: HIGH for mBridge/CIPS; HIGH that BRICS unified alternative is years away**

---

### 3. Petro-Dollar Mechanism: Under Stress, Not Failing

Saudi Arabia reporting suggests a **cancellation of the petrodollar arrangement** (April 2026 reporting on a ~2-year-old decision). Gulf states have been "quietly diversifying" oil trade partners for years outside dollar settlement.

> **Source caveat**: This claim originates primarily from a Fortune tabloid report. Saudi Arabia simultaneously issued record dollar debt ($55B GCC bank securities in 2025) and maintains US security relationships. The behavioral signal is mixed — the directional commitment has shifted, but full petrodollar collapse is not confirmed by conservative sources. Treat this as a directional signal, not a confirmed fact.

Dollar FX reserve share decline from 72% peak to 57% currently represents ~15 percentage points of structural erosion. At current pace, sub-50% is achievable within 5-7 years.

**The dollar's reserve status remains the most durable component** — there is no credible voluntary replacement. Yuan is a geopolitical competitor's currency. Gold is a commodity. SDRs are an accounting unit.

The dollar weaponization paradox is in force: every use of SWIFT as a sanction tool accelerates alternative development.

**Confidence: MEDIUM-HIGH that directional shift is real; MEDIUM on structural magnitude**

### 4. Scenario Analysis

| Scenario              | Probability (5yr) | Description                                                                               |
| --------------------- | ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Base Case (10%)**   | **80%**           | Slow erosion continues; dollar FX share to ~51%, mBridge $200B+ vol                       |
| **Accelerated (30%)** | **17%**           | Catalyst triggers faster shift; dollar FX share to ~42%, mBridge primary Asia-ME corridor |
| **Systemic (50%+)**   | **3%**            | Regime change; dollar loses reserve primacy; US borrowing costs spike 200-400bps          |

> **Red team adjustment**: Original analysis had A(65%), B(25%), C(10%). Red team review increased base-case weight significantly. Rationale: mBridge is real operational infrastructure (not vaporware); capital flow direction is policy-driven; BRICS bypass dynamics are structurally resilient. However, mBridge's $55B cumulative volume represents ~0.2% of global trade — material as early-stage infrastructure but not yet economically significant.

**Phase transition risk**: The dollar system has tipping-point dynamics. Below ~45% dollar FX share, self-reinforcing reverse spirals could emerge. Not happening today — but the 2026-2030 window is the critical observation period.

---

### 5. BRICS Coalition: Bypass Network, Not Alternative System

BRICS represents ~$60T PPP GDP, 40% of world population — real economic weight. But the coalition has **zero consensus on a common currency or settlement asset**. China wants yuan; India wants balance; Brazil wants real; Saudi wants dollar hedging.

BRICS countries are building _parallel_ systems, not a unified alternative:

- China-Russia: Fully dedollarized (CIPS bilateral)
- China-Gulf: mBridge corridor operational
- India-Russia: Growing bilateral settlement

**Intra-BRICS trade** (~$2-3T annually) is where dedollarization is most credible. This is ~8-10% of global trade. Dedollarization of BRICS-internal trade is happening. Dedollarization of global commodity markets is not.

---

### 6. Gulf-China Mutual Dependency Thesis

A fourth thesis has been proposed and red-teamed: Gulf countries building dedollarization infrastructure because China is their primary trade customer and their primary supplier. US (FIC/MIC/TIC) cannot oppose because US tech/defense production depends on Chinese rare earth minerals that flow through Gulf logistics. Mutual dependency is the structural lock.

**What this thesis gets right**:

- China dominates critical mineral _processing_ (85-90% of REE refining, ~65% of cobalt, ~80% of gallium) — not just mining. Even ore from DRC and Australia refines through China.
- Gulf-China trade dependency is real and growing. UAE re-export hub, Saudi oil flows East, Belt and Road connecting Gulf to Chinese supply chains.
- US has not applied secondary sanctions on Gulf mBridge participants — tacit non-interference observable.

**What the red team found wrong or overstated**:

- **USD peg contradiction is fatal**: Saudi riyal (3.75/USD) and UAE dirham (3.67/USD) are pegged to USD. A country on a USD peg cannot simultaneously lead structural dedollarization without abandoning the peg. This thesis never reconciles it.
- **mBridge is de-Westernization, not dedollarization**: mBridge is BIS-hosted and multi-central-bank — not China-controlled. CNY pricing for Gulf-China oil is bilateral trade facilitation. These are different from dollar losing reserve currency status.
- **"US cannot act" overstated**: US has chosen not to apply maximum pressure for strategic reasons (OPEC+ cooperation, Iran counterbalance) unrelated to rare earth dependency. US has SWIFT leverage, banking sanctions, and defense contract leverage that don't involve minerals.
- **US building alternatives**: IRA/BIL have committed $450M+ to domestic rare earth processing. MP Materials (California) and Lynas (Australia under US DoD contract) are operational. US supply chain diversification is underway.

**Red team verdict: 25/50** — structural logic is coherent, empirical support is mixed. The thesis describes a plausible mechanism but conflates de-Westernization with dedollarization, ignores the USD peg contradiction, and overstates US helplessness.

## Bottom Line

**Dedollarization is real. The question is pace and trigger points.**

The supply-chain rerouting thesis is correct: flows are being redirected, infrastructure is being built, reserves are being reallocated. The dollar's role as global reserve currency is entering a **secular decline** — not a collapse, not a cliff, but a slope.

The 2026-2030 window is the critical inflection period. The structural forces are in place. The trigger points are identifiable. The scenario in which this accelerates into a genuine regime change — while still low-probability — is no longer dismissible as narrative.

## Red Team: Outstanding Variables

The following factors were identified by red team review as insufficiently addressed in the base analysis:

**1. US Fiscal Trajectory (critical omitted variable)**
US federal debt path is the single largest swing factor for dollar dominance. If US fiscal position deteriorates beyond ~130% debt-to-GDP with rising real yields, dollar dominance erodes on its own — regardless of what China or BRICS do. This is not incorporated into the scenario model. A US fiscal crisis would dramatically raise the probability of the Systemic scenario.

**2. Capital Account Convertibility — Binding RMB Constraint**
RMB internationalization faces a fundamental constraint: China maintains capital controls. RMB cannot become a true reserve currency without capital account liberalization — which would require China to surrender monetary sovereignty. This is a structural ceiling on yuan's global role that the base analysis underweights. Most RMB internationalization to date has been trade invoicing, not reserve accumulation.

**3. Digital Dollar / FedNow Response**
The US has not deployed a meaningful CBDC response. FedNow (2023) is domestic-only. A digital dollar as a cross-border payment instrument would dramatically extend dollar infrastructure advantages. The base analysis assumes US inaction — a reasonable assumption given current policy inertia, but a significant wildcard if US monetary architecture policy shifts.

**4. Crisis Dollar Strengthening Pattern**
Every global crisis (2008, 2020, 2022) sees dollar demand surge as the world's safe asset of last resort. This "flight-to-safety" dynamic temporarily reverses dedollarization trends and is not captured in the scenario model. The base case assumes peaceful continued erosion — not a crisis scenario where dollar temporarily reasserts.
