# Capital Flows: US Treasuries, Gold, and Reserve Composition

## Research Question

Are China and GCC states structurally reducing US Treasury exposure and accumulating alternative assets?

---

## 1. China: The Long Treasury Exit

**Peak-to-Present Decline**

| Period               | US Treasury Holdings | Change             |
| -------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------ |
| November 2013 (peak) | $1,316.7B            | —                  |
| December 2024        | $759B                | -42% from peak     |
| End 2025             | $638.5B              | -16% from Dec 2024 |
| February 2026        | $693.3B              | Slight rebound     |

**Annual Reduction Pace (from thepaper.cn, via Global Times, Nov 2025)**

- 2022: -$173.2B
- 2023: not disclosed in aggregate
- 2024: -$57.3B
- Late 2024–2025: accelerated selling

**Current Status (April 2026)**

- China has been a **net seller for 9 consecutive months** (Barron's, Jan 2026)
- Lowest Treasury holdings since September 2008
- Chinese regulators **directly urging** domestic financial institutions to reduce US Treasury exposure (Atlantic Council, Feb 2026)
- This is now policy, not just market dynamics

**Key Nuance**: "China sells — rest of world buys." Foreign holdings of US Treasuries remain large; the US has not faced a dramatic forced selling event. China's exit has been absorbed by Japanese, UK, and European institutional buyers — so far.

---

## 2. GCC States: More Complex Picture

**Gulf Bank Dollar Debt Issuance (Fitch Ratings, Sep 2025)**

- GCC bank USD debt issuance: ~$55B in 2025 YTD, already exceeding 2024's $36B
- GCC sovereigns and corporates are **issuing dollar debt at record rates**
- This means GCC entities are _raising_ dollars, not exiting them

**Treasury Holdings — GCC Sovereigns**

- Saudi Arabia (largest GCC economy): significant but not fully disclosed reserve composition
- UAE: active in mBridge, diversifying
- Kuwait, Qatar: less publicly tracked

**The GCC Paradox**: Gulf states are simultaneously:

1. Issuing record dollar debt in international markets
2. Reducing official reserve allocation to dollar assets
3. Accumulating gold (UAE, Saudi Arabia are gold buyers)

This reflects **dual-track behavior**: commercial/financial activity still dollar-denominated, while strategic reserves shift.

## 3. Gold: The Structural Accumulation

**Central Bank Gold Buying — Multi-Decade High**

| Year           | EM Central Bank Purchases   | Notes                                     |
| -------------- | --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| 2022–2024      | >1,000 tonnes/year (record) | Unprecedented streak                      |
| 2025 full year | ~634 tonnes                 | Discovery Alert; ~860 tonnes per Substack |
| H1 2025        | 415 tonnes                  | -21% vs H1 2024 (Amundi)                  |

**Top EM Buyers (2020–2025, tonnes)**

| Rank | Country | Net Additions |
| ---- | ------- | ------------- |
| 1    | China   | +357          |
| 2    | Poland  | +315          |
| 3    | Turkey  | +252          |
| 4    | India   | +245          |

**Leading Buyers 2025 alone**: Brazil, Kazakhstan, Poland

**Structural Signal**: A record **43% of central banks** indicated plans to increase gold reserves in 2025 surveys, up from 29% in 2024 (World Gold Council, Jan 2026). **Zero** central banks surveyed said they planned to reduce gold holdings.

**Gold Price**: Multiple record highs throughout 2025, continuing into 2026. The rally is partly driven by central bank demand and has actually caused a _slowdown_ in buying as prices deter some marginal purchasers — but structurally, demand remains elevated.

---

## 4. Reserve Composition: The Slow Erosion

**Dollar Share of Global FX Reserves**

- Peak: 72% (2001)
- Q3 2025: **56.9%** (Herald Star, Mar 2026)
- Decline: ~15 percentage points over 24 years

**What This Means**: The dollar is still dominant by far. 56.9% is still majority control of global reserve currency status. But the **direction is clear and accelerating** — and importantly, this metric is in freefall when you look at _new_ reserve accumulation, not the stock. New reserves being accumulated by EM central banks are predominantly gold and non-dollar currencies.

---

## 5. Synthesis: Structural or Cyclical?

**Structural indicators present:**

- China policy directive to reduce US Treasury exposure (not market noise)
- 9 consecutive months of selling
- Gold buying at historically elevated levels across EM central banks
- Dollar FX reserve share in secular decline

**Counter-evidence (why dollar dominance isn't collapsing):**

- Rest of world still absorbing China's Treasury selling
- GCC issuing dollar debt at record pace
- Dollar still 57% of global FX reserves
- No credible replacement reserve currency exists
- mBridge transactions ($55B) are a rounding error vs. $100T+ in global FX markets

**Assessment**: **Structural shift, early stage.** The "slow erosion" scenario — not a sudden "dedollarization event" — is the most likely trajectory.
