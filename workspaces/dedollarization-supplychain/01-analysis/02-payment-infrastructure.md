# Payment Infrastructure: mBridge, CIPS, and the SWIFT Alternatives

## Research Question

Is the infrastructure for dedollarized trade settlement ready for primetime?

---

## 1. Project mBridge: The Flagship Alternative

**What It Is**
A multi-CBDC wholesale platform for cross-border payments, built by the BIS Innovation Hub with China, Hong Kong, UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Thailand as core participants.

**Current Status (April 2026)**

| Metric                        | Value                                  |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| MVP reached                   | Mid-2024                               |
| Cumulative transaction volume | **>$55 billion**                       |
| Number of transactions        | **>4,000**                             |
| Volume growth since 2022      | **~2,500x**                            |
| Participating jurisdictions   | China, HK, Saudi Arabia, Thailand, UAE |

**BIS Official Statement (Nov 2024)**: "Project mBridge reached the minimum viable product (MVP) stage in mid-2024."

**Why This Matters**
mBridge is not theoretical. It is settling real, live transactions between sovereign CBDCs across borders — bypassing SWIFT, bypassing the dollar intermediate settlement layer entirely.

**The Volume Reality Check**
$55B in cumulative transactions sounds large. In context:

- Daily global FX settlement: ~$7.5 trillion (BIS data)
- Annual global trade: ~$32 trillion
- $55B is ~0.2% of annual trade — but the **growth rate** is what matters
- 2,500x growth since 2022 suggests exponential adoption curve

**What mBridge Settles**: Primarily trade finance transactions — the core use case for international commerce between mBridge members.

## 2. China's CIPS: Yuan-Denominated Settlement Infrastructure

**CIPS (Cross-Border Interbank Payment System)**

| Metric                           | Value                                                       |
| -------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Launched                         | 2015                                                        |
| Indirect participants (Jan 2025) | **1,467**                                                   |
| Function                         | RMB-denominated cross-border payments, independent of SWIFT |

**Key Point**: CIPS is _not_ a messaging system like SWIFT — it's a payment system. It handles clearing and settlement directly. SWIFT is the messaging layer; CIPS is the actual money movement for RMB transactions.

**CIPS Adoption**: Growing steadily. As of 2025, CIPS handles a meaningful share of China's international RMB payments. China-Russia trade is increasingly settled via CIPS and local currency swap lines, bypassing dollar entirely.

**Strategic Significance**: CIPS makes RMB internationalization _operationally possible_. Without it, even if countries wanted to settle in yuan, they couldn't clear the transactions.

---

## 3. Russia's SPFS: Sanctions-Driven Alternative

**SPFS (System for Transfer of Financial Messages)**

- Russia's response to being expelled from SWIFT in 2022 (part of Western sanctions)
- Designed as a domestic replacement for SWIFT messaging
- Used for Russia-China trade, Russia-India trade in local currencies
- Transaction volumes still dwarfed by SWIFT

**Strategic significance**: Russia's isolation has inadvertently accelerated dedollarization by proving the concept works at scale under pressure. Russia has demonstrated that a major economy can function without SWIFT, giving other countries the confidence to diversify.

---

## 4. BRICS Pay: Proposed but Not Operational

**Status (as of April 2026)**: Announced at the 16th BRICS Summit in Kazan (2024). Framework exists. **No fully unified BRICS payment system is operational.**

**Why the Delay**: BRICS members have deeply conflicting interests:

- China wants yuan dominance
- India wants rupee/rupee-yuan balance
- Brazil wants real
- Gulf states want dollar flexibility
- No consensus on a common denominator currency

**What BRICS Pay Would Need to Work**:

1. A common settlement layer (currently absent)
2. Political consensus among 11 members with divergent interests
3. Significant infrastructure buildout
4. Trust framework for cross-central-bank settlement

---

## 5. SWIFT's Position: Still Dominant, But No Longer Unchallenged

| System                          | Daily Transactions  | Global Share                  |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| SWIFT                           | ~45 million         | ~80% of cross-border payments |
| mBridge (cumulative since 2022) | ~4,000 transactions | <0.1% of global trade         |

**SWIFT's Moat Is Eroding Slowly**

- US has weaponized SWIFT access as a sanction tool (Russia 2022, Iran)
- This has given every non-aligned country a strong incentive to develop alternatives
- SWIFT remains the default — but now there's a credible backup option for countries that want to route around dollar infrastructure

**The Dual-Use Problem**: Even countries building SWIFT alternatives still use SWIFT for most transactions. mBridge is additive, not yet substitutive.

---

## 6. Infrastructure Readiness Assessment

| Component         | Status                                     | Assessment              |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------ | ----------------------- |
| mBridge MVP       | Operational since mid-2024                 | **Early but real**      |
| CIPS              | Operational since 2015, 1,467 participants | **Functional, growing** |
| SPFS              | Operational since 2022                     | **Sanctions-proven**    |
| BRICS Pay         | Proposed, not unified                      | **Not ready**           |
| SWIFT replacement | Does not exist                             | **Years away**          |

**Critical Infrastructure Gap**: There is no **cross-bracket settlement system** that unifies mBridge + CIPS + SPFS into a single global alternative. Countries maintain bilateral arrangements. The next step — a universal settlement layer — requires geopolitical alignment that doesn't yet exist.
