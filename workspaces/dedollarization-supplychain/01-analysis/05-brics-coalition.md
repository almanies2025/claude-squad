# BRICS Coalition: Coordination Capacity and Settlement Reach

## Research Question

Can the BRICS coalition coordinate sufficiently to build a credible alternative financial infrastructure?

## 2. The Fundamental BRICS Contradiction

**BRICS Has No Agreed Reserve Currency** — and cannot while members have conflicting monetary interests:

| Country      | Preferred Outcome         | Why                                                        |
| ------------ | ------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| China        | Yuan dominates            | Largest economy, export-led, controlled capital account    |
| India        | Rupee-yuan balance        | Doesn't want yuan dominance any more than dollar dominance |
| Russia       | Any alternative to dollar | Sanctioned, genuinely motivated                            |
| Brazil       | Real + commodity basket   | Agricultural superpower, doesn't want yuan peg             |
| Saudi Arabia | Dollar + hedging          | Wealthy, dollar-denominated assets, US security guarantee  |
| UAE          | Pragmatic neutrality      | Financial hub; connects both systems                       |
| Indonesia    | Non-aligned               | Largest ASEAN economy, balanced                            |
| Iran         | Anything anti-West        | Sanctioned, motivated                                      |
| South Africa | African interests first   | Commodity exporter, limited alignment                      |

**The Coordination Paradox**: BRICS countries are united by what they are _against_ (dollar dominance) but have zero consensus on what they are _for_ (a common currency or settlement system).

## 3. What BRICS Has Actually Built

**What Exists Today**:

| System                    | Scope                     | Participants                           | Status                           |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| CIPS (China)              | RMB cross-border payments | 1,467 indirect participants globally   | **Operational**                  |
| mBridge (BIS/China-led)   | Multi-CBDC settlement     | China, HK, UAE, Saudi Arabia, Thailand | **Operational, $55B+ volume**    |
| SPFS (Russia)             | SWIFT alternative         | Russia + bilateral partners            | **Operational**                  |
| BRICS Pay                 | Unified BRICS payment     | Proposed                               | **Not unified, not operational** |
| Local Currency Swap Lines | Bilateral settlement      | Various                                | **Growing network**              |

**Key Insight**: BRICS countries are building _parallel_ systems — not a unified alternative. mBridge is not a BRICS institution; it is a BIS-China-UAE-Saudi initiative. Russia uses SPFS. China uses CIPS. The infrastructure is fragmented.

## 4. BRICS Institutional Depth Assessment

**What BRICS Lacks for Real Infrastructure**:

| Requirement                        | Status  | Gap                                            |
| ---------------------------------- | ------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Common settlement layer            | None    | No common currency or settlement asset         |
| Trusted central counterparty       | None    | No BRICS central bank equivalent               |
| Financial regulatory harmonization | Minimal | Legal systems incompatible                     |
| Dispute resolution mechanism       | None    | No BRICS financial court                       |
| Capital account convertibility     | Limited | China, India still restrict capital flows      |
| Trust between members              | Low     | China-India border tensions, Gulf-Iran rivalry |

**BRICS Is a Political Bloc, Not a Financial Union**: The EU took 50+ years to build the euro. BRICS has existed for 15 years and has not built a common financial infrastructure. The institutional gap is enormous.

---

## 5. Settlement Reach: What Can BRICS Actually Settle Today?

**Intra-BRICS Trade (the realistic dedollarization use case)**:

- China-Russia: **Fully dedollarized** — settled in ruble-yuan via CIPS bilateral corridors, no SWIFT needed
- China-India: Growing but constrained — neither fully trusts the other's currency; rupee-yuan trading mechanisms in development
- Russia-Brazil: Growing — commodity for commodity swap arrangements
- China-Gulf: Active — mBridge participants (UAE, Saudi) can settle directly with China in CBDC
- India-Gulf: Dollar remains dominant (limited BRICS infrastructure to bypass)

**Total Intra-BRICS Trade**: ~$2-3T annually. This is where dedollarization is most advanced and most believable.

**BRICS vs. Global Trade**: Intra-BRICS trade is ~8-10% of global trade. Dedollarization of BRICS intra-trade is structurally happening. Dedollarization of global trade (including dollar-denominated commodity markets) is not.

---

## 6. Saudi Arabia's Position: The Critical Variable

**Saudi Arabia is the pivotal BRICS member for dollar system disruption:**

- World's largest oil exporter
- Traditional US security ally (the petrodollar's original architect alongside the US)
- Now a BRICS member
- Active on mBridge

**Saudi Arabia's behavior will determine whether BRICS becomes a genuine alternative or remains a political forum**:

- If Saudi Arabia begins pricing any oil in non-dollar currencies at scale → structural break
- If Saudi Arabia keeps oil in dollars but uses mBridge for bilateral settlements → parallel system, not replacement
- If Saudi Arabia hedges between systems → most likely outcome for near-to-medium term

**Current Saudi behavior**: Issuing record dollar debt (Fitch: $55B in 2025) while simultaneously participating in mBridge. The dual-track strategy.

---

## 7. Verdict: BRICS Capacity Assessment

**BRICS can build**:

- Bilateral dedollarization corridors between aligned pairs (China-Russia: done; China-Gulf: mBridge; India-Russia: growing)
- A parallel infrastructure for BRICS-internal trade
- Narrative and political momentum for dedollarization

**BRICS cannot build** (in any credible near-to-medium term):

- A unified global payment alternative
- A reserve currency to rival the dollar
- An institutional framework that competes with SWIFT/IMF/BIS

**Strategic Conclusion**: BRICS is building a _bypass network_, not an alternative system. Countries that want to route around the dollar for specific corridors now can. But a wholesale replacement of dollar infrastructure requires institutional depth that BRICS will take decades to build — if ever.
