# Dedollarization Supply Chain — Initial Brief

## Product Concept

**Question**: Is dedollarization real? Is there a structural shift in global monetary power, or is it rhetorical?

## Core Observation

A multipolar world is emerging. China and GCC states are reducing their US Treasury holdings and buying gold. BIS's mBridge project is building alternative cross-border payment infrastructure that bypasses SWIFT and the dollar-dominated system. The hypothesis: dedollarization is not a single event but a supply-chain-level rerouting of global trade finance — and it is accelerating.

## Key Data Points

- China + GCC have been net sellers of US Treasuries since ~2022
- Gold reserves at multi-decade highs across emerging market central banks
- mBridge (BIS Project Aurora) operational and growing — real cross-border CBDC transactions
- BRICS expansion creating alternative coordination bloc for trade settlement
- Petro-dollar recycling mechanism showing stress fractures

## Analytical Objective

Assess whether dedollarization is a structural force or cyclical narrative by examining:

1. **Flows**: Who is selling what, to whom, and why?
2. **Infrastructure**: mBridge, CIPS, local currency swap lines — are they ready for primetime?
3. **Adoption**: Which countries are on-ramping? What are they settling?
4. **Resilience**: Can the dollar hold without petrodollar recycling?
5. **Scenarios**: What does a 10% / 30% / 50% dedollarized world look like?

## Scope

Research-first analysis. No product build. Deliverable is a comprehensive research document with scenario modeling and risk assessment.
