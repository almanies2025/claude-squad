## Discovery: Central Bank Gold Accumulation Is Now Structural Policy

**What was found**: A record **43% of central banks** indicated plans to increase gold reserves in 2025 surveys, up from 29% in 2024. **Zero** central banks surveyed planned to reduce gold holdings. EM central bank gold buying:

- 2022-2024: >1,000 tonnes/year (unprecedented streak)
- 2025: ~630-860 tonnes (still historically elevated)

**Top buyers 2020-2025**: China (+357t), Poland (+315t), Turkey (+252t), India (+245t). Leading buyers in 2025 alone: Brazil, Kazakhstan, Poland.

**Why it matters**: This is not speculative buying. Central banks are price-elastic but structurally motivated. When 43% of the world's central banks say they're buying more gold — and zero say they're selling — the trend is institutionally embedded.

**Key insight**: The price rally in 2025 (gold up 26%) has actually _slowed_ some buying (price deterrence effect). But demand remains at historically elevated levels despite price headwinds — suggesting the structural demand is strong enough to override price sensitivity.

**What this means for the thesis**: Gold is functioning as the **neutral reserve asset** in a multipolar monetary world. Central banks diversifying away from dollar assets don't want yuan, don't trust the euro as a reserve, and find gold to be the only politically neutral alternative. This is a structural demand source that will persist regardless of near-term dollar dynamics.

**Sources**: World Gold Council (Jan 2026), Amundi Research, Discovery Alert, Substack (The People's Economist)
