## Discovery: Saudi Arabia Has Already Canceled the Petrodollar Deal

**What was found**: Saudi Arabia **quietly canceled its petrodollar arrangement** with the United States — reporting emerged April 2026 but the decision is approximately 2 years old. Gulf countries have been "quietly diversifying their trade partners" for years, trading oil outside the dollar system.

**Why it matters**: Saudi Arabia was the original architect of the petrodollar system alongside the US in 1974. It was the linchpin that made the arrangement credible because it was the world's swing oil producer with the most to lose from dollar instability. **The linchpin has moved.**

**Critical nuance**: This doesn't mean the petrodollar is dead. Saudi Arabia is still pricing oil in dollars for most contracts, still issuing record dollar debt ($55B in 2025), and still maintaining US security relationships. But the _directional commitment_ has changed.

**The dual-track problem for the US**: Gulf states are simultaneously:

1. Participating in mBridge (bypassing dollar settlement)
2. Issuing record dollar debt (demanding dollar liquidity)
3. Accumulating gold (reducing reserve allocation to dollar assets)

This is not contradictory — it's hedging. But it means the "perpetual buyer" of Treasuries is transitioning from a policy-aligned partner to a pragmatic hedger.

**Sources**: Fortune (Apr 2026), Capital Street FX, Market Architect Capital Research (jinlow Substack)
