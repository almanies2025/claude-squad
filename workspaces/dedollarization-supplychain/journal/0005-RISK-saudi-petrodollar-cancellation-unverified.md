## RISK: Saudi Petrodollar Cancellation Claim Is Unverified and Contradicted by Behavior

**What was found**: The analysis at 03-petrodollar-mechanism.md cites Fortune (April 2026) as evidence that "Saudi Arabia cancelled its petrodollar arrangement with the US." This claim was described as a "2-year-old decision" — meaning it was made around April 2024. However:

1. No official Saudi Arabian government statement confirming this policy change exists
2. No official US Treasury or State Department confirmation
3. Saudi Arabia simultaneously issued a record $55B in dollar debt in 2025 (per Fitch)
4. Saudi Arabia has maintained its currency peg to the dollar throughout this period
5. Fortune has a tabloid financial reputation — not an authoritative policy source

**Why it matters**: This is the "specific structural break" the thesis hinges on in 03-petrodollar-mechanism.md. If the Saudi cancellation is unverified or misreported, the linchpin break disappears and the petro-dollar mechanism reverts to "under stress but not failing" — which is the analysis's own assessment before this claim.

**The pattern**: The analysis treats an unverified media report as a confirmed policy change. The more reliable indicators (dollar debt issuance, oil pricing behavior, FX peg) all point to continuity, not change.

**Source quality hierarchy** (what should have been cited):

- Official Saudi Press Agency or US-State Department statements
- IMF Article IV consultations with Saudi Arabia
- Financial Times or Wall Street Journal with named official sources
- Not Fortune magazine anonymous-source reporting on a "2-year-old decision"
