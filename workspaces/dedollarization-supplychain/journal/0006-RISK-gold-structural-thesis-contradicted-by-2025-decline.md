## RISK: Gold Accumulation Thesis Overstated — 2025 Decline Contradicts Structural Narrative

**What was found**: The analysis presents gold accumulation by EM central banks as a structural shift in reserve composition. Key data:

- 2022-2024: >1,000 tonnes/year (record streak)
- 2025 full year: ~630-860 tonnes (per Amundi: -21% vs H1 2024)
- 2026: Multiple record gold prices have actually _deterrred_ some marginal buyers

**The structural interpretation**: "Record gold buying represents a structural diversification away from dollar assets."

**The cyclical interpretation**: 2022-2024 buying was driven by:

- Post-2022 geopolitical shock (Ukraine war, sanctions) creating safe-haven demand
- Negative real yields in Western bonds making gold attractive
- Diversification from frozen Russian reserves (not from dollars specifically)
- Dollar pricing dynamics (gold is dollar-denominated — buying gold IS holding dollars in commodity form)

**Why the 2025 decline matters**: If gold accumulation were structural, why did purchases decline significantly in 2025? The analysis attributes this to "price deterrence" — meaning high prices are stopping buyers. This is the opposite of structural demand, which intensifies at higher prices.

**What the analysis missed**: The WGC survey (43% plan to increase gold) measures _intentions_, not behavior. Central bank surveys consistently overstate planned diversification. Actual behavior (2025 decline) contradicts the stated intentions.

**Assessment**: The gold buying thesis is more consistent with cyclical geopolitical hedging than structural reserve diversification. The analysis should not weight gold as heavily as it does.

## For Discussion

3. The analysis says gold buying is "structurally elevated" in 2025 at ~630-860 tonnes. What threshold would indicate the structural thesis is wrong? (Below 500 tonnes/year sustained?)
