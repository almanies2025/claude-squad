## GAP: Missing Analysis of US Fiscal Trajectory as Dedollarization Trigger

**What was found**: The analysis at 04-scenario-modeling.md identifies "US fiscal crisis" as a potential catalyst for Scenario B/C (30%/50% dedollarization) but treats it as one of several equal-probability triggers. The US fiscal position is not analyzed as a base-case variable.

**The gap**: US fiscal trajectory is arguably the MOST LIKELY trigger for dedollarization — and also the most underanalyzed:

- US federal deficit in 2025: ~$1.8-2.0T (6-7% of GDP)
- US debt-to-GDP: ~120-125% and rising
- Interest payments on debt: now the largest federal expenditure, surpassing defense
- No political consensus on fiscal consolidation
- Treasury market has shown signs of stress (2022 yield spike, 2025 long-end weakness)

**Why this matters for the thesis**: The analysis claims dedollarization is "structural" but the primary mechanism for accelerated dedollarization (Scenario C, 50%+ dedollarization) is explicitly a US fiscal crisis — which is not structural dedollarization at all. It would be dollar weakness driven by US domestic fiscal mismanagement, not by the success of alternatives.

**The distinction**: If US fiscal crisis causes dedollarization, this validates the thesis only if:

1. The crisis is severe enough to destroy confidence permanently
2. Alternatives are ready to absorb the displaced dollar demand
3. The US cannot recover fiscal credibility

If US fiscal consolidation occurs (through political compromise or crisis), the dedollarization thesis weakens significantly.

**What the analysis should include**:

- US fiscal baseline projection (2026-2030) under current policy
- Sensitivity analysis: at what debt/GDP level does Treasury market stress become self-reinforcing?
- Historical parallel: UK gilts crisis 2022 (temporary pound weakness, no structural shift)
- Historical parallel: US 2008 financial crisis (dollar STRENGTHENED, dedollarization thesis falsified)

## For Discussion

1. Is a US fiscal crisis (triggering Scenario C) consistent with the analysis's "structural" framing? Or does it prove dedollarization is externally triggered by US policy failures rather than the success of alternatives?
2. If US fiscal crisis occurs but alternatives are not ready, what happens? (Likely: dollar temporarily weakens, then recovers as US fiscal credibility is restored.)
3. What is the probability of US fiscal crisis in the 2026-2030 window? The analysis should assign a probability and model it explicitly, not treat it as one of several equal triggers.
