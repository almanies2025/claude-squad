## Risk: Dollar Weaponization Feedback Loop Thesis Has Critical Unsupported Assertion

**Red team verdict**: The thesis scores **5/10** overall. The mechanism is internally coherent but several links are empirically weak or contradicted by recent data. The political economy equilibrium claim (Link 8: "US will always choose MIC over dollar stability") is the **weakest and most critical link** — entirely asserted, not demonstrated.

### The Core Problem: Link 8

The thesis requires the political economy to stably prefer MIC spending over dollar stability. But:

- **2022-2024 directly contradicts this**: Fed raised rates 500bps in 18 months despite intense MIC lobbying and political pressure
- **Sequestration (2013)**: Defense spending was cut when fiscal pressure mounted
- **Defense spending real terms**: Flat to declining 2010-2020 post-surge
- **2022 midterm inflation anger**: Political system responded with rate hikes, not MIC expansion

The thesis **assumes what it needs to prove** — that the equilibrium always favors MIC. This is motivated reasoning.

---

### Five Falsification Conditions

If ANY of these are true, the thesis collapses:

1. **Fed autonomy prevails under pressure** — 2022-2024 already falsifies this partially
2. **Treasury market absorbs foreign selling without yield spike** — private sector absorbs ~$700B China selling without Fed intervention
3. **No correlation between sanctions frequency and alternative infrastructure** — mBridge correlates with BRICS strategic drift, not US SWIFT usage
4. **Dollar FX reserve share stable at ~58-60%** — flat since 2016 despite alternative growth
5. **US fiscal path without monetization** — current spending sustained through non-monetary means

---

### What Validates the Thesis

The thesis describes a **plausible failure mode**, not current state. To validate, you would need:

- Fed balance sheet **expanding** while foreign holdings fall (not happening — QT since 2022)
- Dollar FX reserve share **meaningfully below 58%** (flat since 2016)
- Treasury yields **rising** despite Fed QT (not happening — yields fell 2022-2024 then stabilized)
- **Sustained** political capitulation to MIC over inflation control (2022-2024 shows the opposite)

---

### Key Distinction: Structural Possibility vs. Empirical Actuality

The thesis conflates:

- **What could happen** under sustained pressure (structural possibility)
- **What is happening** (empirical actuality)

The feedback loop is a **sword of Damocles that has not fallen**. The structural pressures are real, but the political economy equilibrium has not (yet) chosen MIC over dollar stability — the 2022-2024 period shows the opposite.

---

### Implications for Dedollarization Analysis

Three scenarios for how this thesis should inform the broader analysis:

**Scenario A ( thesis correct)**: Dollar weaponization feedback loop is the PRIMARY dedollarization mechanism. Probability of systemic scenario increases if US fiscal position deteriorates further. Fed monetization begins. MIC spending accelerates.

**Scenario B ( thesis partially correct)**: The feedback loop is a tail risk — not active but structurally possible. The sword of Damocles is real but not yet falling. Base case (slow erosion) remains dominant.

**Scenario C ( thesis wrong direction)**: The feedback loop does not activate because Fed autonomy and political constraint on spending are stronger than the thesis assumes. Dedollarization proceeds via bilateral bypass (mBridge, CIPS), not via US fiscal self-undermining.
