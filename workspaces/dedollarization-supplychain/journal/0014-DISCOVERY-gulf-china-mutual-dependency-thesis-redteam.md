## Discovery: Gulf-China Mutual Dependency Thesis Red Team Results

**Thesis scored 25/50 — moderate credibility, structurally coherent but empirically patchy**

---

### What Was Red Teamed

**The thesis**: Gulf countries building dedollarization infrastructure because China is their primary trade customer. FIC/MIC/TIC cannot stop it because US tech/defense depends on Chinese rare earth minerals that flow through Gulf logistics. Mutual dependency is the lock.

---

### What Validated as Accurate

1. **China rare earth processing dominance is real and critical**
   - ~85-90% of global REE (rare earth elements) processing
   - ~65% of cobalt refining (ore from DRC, refined in China)
   - ~80% of gallium, ~60% of germanium
   - Key insight: China's dominance is in _processing_, not mining. Even ore from DRC and Australia flows through Chinese refineries.
   - US Geological Survey: China supplies ~75-80% of US rare earth imports (processed compounds, not raw ore)

2. **Gulf-China trade dependency is real and growing**
   - Saudi oil flows East — documented increase in Saudi exports to China
   - UAE re-export hub connecting China to Middle East/Africa
   - Belt and Road infrastructure connecting Gulf to Chinese supply chains

3. **Structural stress on US-Gulf alignment is real**
   - mBridge participation without US sanctions
   - No secondary sanctions applied to Saudi/UAE for mBridge participation

---

### What Failed Red Team

#### 1. USD peg contradiction — FATAL and unaddressed

Saudi Riyal (SAR) pegged at 3.75 per USD. UAE Dirham (AED) pegged at 3.67 per USD.

A country on a USD peg cannot simultaneously lead structural dedollarization without abandoning the peg. The thesis never reconciles this.

**Possible resolutions (not explored by thesis)**:

- Dual-track system: most Gulf trade settles USD via peg; only China bilateral trade uses alternatives
- Pegs are transitional: building alternatives now, will depegg when infrastructure is ready
- What thesis calls "dedollarization" is actually bilateral trade facilitation with China, not systemic dollar replacement

#### 2. mBridge volume is marginal

BIS data: ~$1.1B cumulative mBridge transactions. Against trillions in Gulf-US trade, this is functionally negligible.

Thesis conflates: building infrastructure ≠ operational dedollarization

#### 3. "US cannot act" overstated

US has chosen not to apply maximum pressure for strategic reasons (OPEC+ oil cooperation, Iran counterbalance) not purely about rare earths.

US has leverage that doesn't require minerals: SWIFT access, banking sanctions, defense contract leverage, oil market flooding threat.

#### 4. US building alternative supply chains (thesis ignores)

IRA + BIL: $450M+ in rare earth processing subsidies. MP Materials (Mountain Pass, California) operational. Lynas (Australia) under US DoD contract. Thesis uses 2022 data as if static.

#### 5. Dedollarization ≠ de-Westernization (critical distinction)

mBridge is BIS-hosted, multi-central-bank — not China-controlled infrastructure. CNY oil pricing ≠ dedollarization of monetary system.

Thesis conflates three distinct phenomena:

- Bilateral currency swaps (China-Gulf)
- Non-USD commodity pricing (CNY oil contracts)
- CBDC infrastructure (mBridge)

These have different implications for dollar dominance.

---

### Falsification Conditions

1. **US alternative supply chains >40% operational by 2030** — MP Materials output, Lynas deliveries, DOE stockpile data
2. **US applies credible secondary sanctions on Gulf mBridge participants** — OFAC designation, Swift removal threat
3. **Gulf breaks or abandons USD pegs** — SAR or AED announces floating or currency basket
4. **Gulf-China trade share reverses** — 3+ consecutive years of declining China share of Gulf trade
5. **mBridge remains marginal** — <$5B cumulative, no new joiners, no commercial bank adoption

---

### Key Analytical Distinction

**Dedollarization**: USD loses reserve currency status, replaced by another currency or basket

**De-Westernization**: Alternatives built to Western-controlled infrastructure (SWIFT, CHIPS), but settlement may still touch USD liquidity or use multi-currency rails

mBridge is better characterized as de-Westernization. This is real and significant, but different from "dollar being replaced."

---

### For Discussion

1. Is the thesis describing dedollarization (dollar being replaced) or de-Westernization (alternatives to Western-controlled infrastructure)? These have very different implications for the scenario model probabilities.

2. The USD peg means Gulf is running a dual-track system. Is the thesis actually about building _options_ (reducing dependency in extremis) rather than active dedollarization? This would change the probability assessment significantly.

3. If the 10-year trajectory shows mBridge infrastructure built but transaction volumes still marginal AND pegs maintained — does the thesis fail? What is the thesis actually predicting?
