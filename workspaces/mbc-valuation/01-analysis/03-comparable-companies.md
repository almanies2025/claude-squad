# Comparable Companies Analysis — MBC Group (4072.SR)

**Date:** April 24, 2026 | **Analyst:** MBA Practice Exercise

---

## Why Comparable Companies Works

The DCF required us to project 5 years into the future and estimate a terminal growth rate — both deeply uncertain for a company in transition. Trading comparables bypass that by asking: **what does the market pay today for similar companies?**

The logic is simple: investors in comparable companies have already priced in growth, risk, and margin expectations. MBC should trade in line with its peers — unless there's a specific reason it deserves a premium or discount.

---

## Peer Group Construction

MBC has three distinct businesses. Each deserves a different peer set:

**Challenge:** No pure MENA streaming peer exists on public markets. MBC is the only listed pan-Arab pure-play. This makes the analysis directional rather than precise.

## Step 1 — Trading Multiples Applied to MBC

### Option A: Apply Streaming Multiples (Netflix/Disney)

If MBC is priced like a streaming growth story:

| Multiple Applied | NTM EBITDA (SAR 692.8M) | Implied EV | Less: Net Debt | Implied Equity Value | Implied Share Price | vs. Market |
| ---------------- | ----------------------- | ---------- | -------------- | -------------------- | ------------------- | ---------- |
| Netflix 26x      | 26.3x                   | 18,220     | (1,231)        | **19,451**           | **SAR 58.5**        | +123%      |
| Disney 20x       | 20x                     | 13,856     | (1,231)        | **15,087**           | **SAR 45.4**        | +73%       |

**Problem:** Netflix is not a fair comparison. Netflix has 325M subscribers, $47B in revenue, high-20s operating margins, and is cash-flow positive with massive FCF. MBC's Shahid streaming is ~23% of revenue and likely still margin-negative. Using Netflix multiples inflates MBC's value dramatically.

## Step 3 — Precedent Transactions (Quick Check)

The only recent M&A in this peer set is the **Paramount acquisition by Skydance/National Media** at **7.5x fully synergized 2026 EBITDA**.

Key lesson: strategic acquirers pay a premium for control and synergies. Public market investors in MBC are pricing it as a growth story, not as an acquisition target. If a strategic buyer (Saudi PIF, or a regional media group) were to acquire MBC, the precedent would suggest **7.5x–10x EBITDA** at minimum:

| Multiple         | Applied to MBC NTM EBITDA (692.8M) | Equity Value | Share Price |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------- |
| 7.5x (Paramount) | → EV 5,196                         | SAR 5,427    | SAR 16.3    |
| 10x              | → EV 6,928                         | SAR 7,159    | SAR 21.5    |
| 12x              | → EV 8,314                         | SAR 9,545    | SAR 28.7    |

**This is a floor, not a ceiling** — it represents what a strategic buyer would pay, which tends to be higher than public market valuation for growing companies.

## Key Analytical Findings

### Finding 1: MBC Trades at a Discount to Streaming Peers

At 11.1x NTM EBITDA, MBC is priced like a **transitioning media company** — not a pure streaming growth story. This makes intuitive sense:

- Shahid is growing fast (34% in 4Q25) but is a small fraction of total revenue
- BOCA (54% of revenue) is a mature advertising business with lower margins
- The market is not yet pricing Shahid at Netflix multiples

### Finding 2: The Market's Implied Shahid Probability

The market prices MBC at 11.1x NTM EBITDA. If the market correctly prices BOCA at ~8x EBITDA (mature media) and assigns Netflix-like multiples to Shahid's contribution...

Rough split at current price:

- BOCA (54% of rev): ~SAR 4,500M contribution at 8x = SAR 562M equity credit
- Shahid (23% of rev): ~SAR 1,900M contribution at... what multiple?
- M&E (23% of rev): ~SAR 1,900M contribution at... what multiple?

At SAR 8,844M market cap, Shahid is essentially being valued for free if BOCA explains most of the equity value. This is the key insight: **the market is assigning near-zero growth premium to Shahid**, which creates an option-like upside for investors who believe in Vision 2030 streaming penetration.

### Finding 3: The Paramount Precedent Is a Floor, Not a Ceiling

Paramount was acquired at 7.5x at a time when Paramount+ had more subscribers than Shahid has today. MBC's balance sheet is far stronger (net cash vs. Paramount's net debt). This suggests the public market is underpricing MBC relative to what a strategic would pay.

## For Discussion

2. The Paramount acquisition happened at 7.5x. MBC is in a stronger financial position (net cash vs. Paramount's debt). Should MBC deserve a higher multiple than 7.5x as a public market standalone — or does the strategic premium in that deal mean the public market simply doesn't care?
