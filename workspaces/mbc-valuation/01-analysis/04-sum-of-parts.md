# Sum-of-Parts Valuation — MBC Group (4072.SR)

**Date:** April 24, 2026 | **Analyst:** MBA Practice Exercise

---

## Why Sum-of-Parts

MBC has three businesses with radically different growth profiles, margin structures, and peer multiples. A single blended multiple obscures this. Sum-of-parts (SOTP) asks: **what is each segment worth if it were traded separately?**

The logic is especially compelling here: Shahid (streaming) is priced by the market as if it will never be profitable, while BOCA (advertising) is mature but cash-generative. The market treats the whole as worse than the sum of its parts — a classic conglomerate discount scenario.

## Step 1 — BOCA Valuation

**Business profile:** Pan-Arab free-to-air television, advertising revenue, mature market position, dominant in KSA and broader MENA.

**Best analog:** Regional/global TV broadcasters — particularly Sinclair Broadcast Group (SBGI), but also WBD's linear TV assets, and regional ad-supported media.

**Key characteristics:**

- High operating margins (~22–24%)
- Decline in linear TV is a long-run concern, but pan-Arab market is less penetrated than US
- Advertising revenue correlated with Saudi/KSA economic cycles
- Near-monopoly position in Arabic-language pan-Arab broadcasting

## Step 2 — Shahid Valuation

**Business profile:** MENA's leading Arabic-language OTT streaming platform. Subscription + ad-supported tiers. 34% revenue growth in 4Q25. Still operating at a loss.

**Best analog:** Netflix (NFLX), Disney+ (DIS), Paramount+ (PARA), but with MENA-specific growth and profitability risk.

**Key characteristics:**

- Clear market leader in Arabic-language streaming — no equivalent MENA competitor
- Revenue growth 34% (4Q25) but EBITDA deeply negative
- Path to profitability: scale economies on content cost, subscriber ARPU improvement
- Vision 2030 tailwind: Saudi digitalization, mobile-first consumption
- Risk: Netflix and Disney+ are expanding into MENA

**Peer multiples for Streaming:**

| Peer                         | NTM EV/EBITDA | Notes                                   |
| ---------------------------- | ------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Netflix                      | 26x           | Hypergrowth, global scale, FCF positive |
| Disney                       | 20x           | Bundle, parks, advertising recovery     |
| Paramount+ (pre-acquisition) | ~15–20x       | Smaller, less profitable                |

**Shahid-specific discount:** Shahid is not yet profitable. We must use EV/Revenue, then benchmark implied EV/EBITDA.

|                          | Low       | Mid       | High       |
| ------------------------ | --------- | --------- | ---------- |
| Shahid Revenue (FY2026E) | 1,500     | 1,650     | 1,800      |
| Revenue growth (implied) | 21%       | 33%       | 45%        |
| EV/Revenue multiple      | 3x        | 5x        | 8x         |
| **Shahid EV (SAR M)**    | **4,500** | **8,250** | **14,400** |

**Cross-check at implied EV/EBITDA:**

If Shahid reaches 15% EBITDA margin at FY2029 (consistent with Disney+ trajectory), EBITDA = SAR 247M.

At 25% margin (Netflix-like at scale): EBITDA = SAR 412M.
At 25x → EV = SAR 10,300M — between mid and high.

## Step 5 — Conglomerate Discount Analysis

The sum-of-parts shows MBC may be worth more as a collection of businesses than as a single entity. This is the **conglomerate discount** — the discount applied by markets to diversified groups.

**Evidence of conglomerate discount:**

At current market price (SAR 26.26), the market is valuing the entire company at SAR 8,844M equity. Our SOTP base case implies SAR 16,441M — a **86% premium** to market.

**Why does the discount exist?**

| Factor              | Explanation                                                                                       |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Complexity discount | Three different businesses are hard to analyze; most analysts cover media, not MENA multi-segment |
| Tracking complexity | No segment-level EBITDA disclosure — market can't accurately value each piece                     |
| Governance concerns | Related-party content deals between segments (Shahid licensing from BOCA production)              |
| Liquidity           | Small float (~332M shares), institutional investors prefer pure-plays                             |

**Historical precedent:** MBC's largest shareholder is PIF (Saudi sovereign wealth fund). PIF may be a patient, strategic holder — not a pure financial investor optimizing for standalone value.

## Key Analytical Findings

### Finding 1: Shahid Drives 50%+ of SOTP Value in Base Case

In our base case (Shahid at 5x EV/Revenue), Shahid represents ~SAR 7,967M of SAR 16,441M total equity = **48% of total value**. Yet Shahid is only 23% of revenue and is not yet EBITDA positive.

This means: **MBC is actually a streaming call option priced at a fraction of its streaming value.** If Shahid reaches Netflix-like margins (20%+), the equity value could double from current levels. The market appears to be pricing Shahid at near-zero, which is either a massive discount for streaming risk — or a mispricing.

### Finding 2: BOCA Finances Shahid's Growth

The broadcasting business (BOCA) generates strong cash flow (~SAR 650M EBITDA) that funds Shahid's content and technology investment. At current market pricing, BOCA's equity contribution (SAR 8,057M base) exceeds MBC's total market cap (SAR 8,844M). This means Shahid and M&E are essentially being given away for free — an extreme version of the conglomerate discount.

### Finding 3: M&E Is the Weakest Segment

M&E (23% of revenue) contributes only ~SAR 417M equity in our base case (~2.5% of total). At 10x EBITDA on thin 5% margins, this segment barely moves the needle. Its primary value may be as an embedded option on Saudi entertainment sector growth rather than a standalone asset.

### Finding 4: Strategic Value vs. Market Value

If MBC were acquired by a strategic buyer (PIF, regional media group), they could justify paying for Shahid's growth trajectory and BOCA's cash generation separately. The SOTP analysis suggests a strategic buyer might pay SAR 35–50 per share — consistent with the DCF base case (SAR 48.8). The public market is not currently applying this logic.

## SOTP vs. Other Methods

| Valuation Method    | Implied Share Price | Notes                                    |
| ------------------- | ------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| **SOTP Base**       | **SAR 49.4**        | BOCA 10.5x + Shahid 5x + M&E 10x         |
| DCF Base Case       | SAR 48.8            | Close alignment — validates both methods |
| Comps Blended       | SAR 35.0            | Conservative (12–18x blended)            |
| Paramount Precedent | SAR 16.3            | Acquisition floor, not standalone value  |
| Market Price        | SAR 26.26           | 44% below SOTP base                      |

**The convergence of SOTP (SAR 49.4) and DCF (SAR 48.8) is notable.** Both independent methods — one based on discounted cash flows, the other on relative value of comparable businesses — arrive at essentially the same point. This is a strong signal that SAR 48–50 is the true fundamental value, and the current market price of SAR 26.26 represents a significant discount.
