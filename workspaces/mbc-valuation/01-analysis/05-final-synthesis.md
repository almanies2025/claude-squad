## The Investment Thesis

### What We Own: Three Businesses, One Discount

MBC Group is three businesses:

1. **BOCA** — The cash engine. Pan-Arab television, dominant market position, ~22% EBITDA margins, generating SAR 650M+ EBITDA annually. Worth SAR 7,000–9,500M as a standalone asset. The market attributes roughly SAR 8,057M to this segment — which is almost equal to MBC's entire market cap. This means Shahid and M&E are essentially free.

2. **Shahid** — The growth option. MENA's leading Arabic streaming platform with 34% revenue growth. Still EBITDA-negative but approaching scale. In our SOTP, Shahid represents SAR 7,967M of equity value (48% of total) at a 5x EV/Revenue multiple. The market appears to be assigning near-zero value to this growth option.

3. **M&E** — The cyclical supplement. Production, events, content licensing. Thin margins (~5%), ~SAR 417M in our base SOTP. Not a driver of value but a stable contributor.

### Why the Discount Exists

The market prices MBC at 11x NTM EBITDA. This is a **conglomerate discount** — the market doesn't reward complexity. Three things suppress the price:

1. **No analyst coverage.** MBC listed January 2024. Few international analysts cover it yet. Information asymmetry means the market prices it as a "trust us" story rather than a fundamentals story.

2. **Streaming skepticism.** The market has watched Netflix squeeze margins for 5 years but hasn't seen an Arab streaming platform do the same. Shahid's path to profitability is theoretically sound but unproven.

3. **Small float.** PIF owns ~60%+ of the company. The free float is thin, which suppresses institutional buying and keeps liquidity low.

### The Re-Rating Catalysts

Four things could close the gap between SAR 26 and SAR 47:

| Catalyst                         | Timing       | Impact                                                                        |
| -------------------------------- | ------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| FY2026 EBITDA margin hits 12%+   | 6–12 months  | Confirms streaming scale economics; supports DCF assumptions                  |
| International analyst initiation | 6–18 months  | Coverage by MSCI EM media analysts raises visibility                          |
| Shahid subscriber milestone      | 12–24 months | 5M+ subscribers triggers re-rating to streaming multiples                     |
| Strategic transaction            | 12–36 months | Paramount precedent (7.5x) suggests strategic buyers would pay 40–60% premium |

### The Bear Case (SAR 20–32)

If Shahid fails to scale — subscriber growth stalls, Netflix dominates MENA, and BOCA's advertising revenue contracts with the Saudi economy — the stock could trade at 8–10x NTM EBITDA, implying SAR 20–25. This scenario assumes:

- Shahid revenue growth slows to 10%
- BOCA EBITDA compresses to 18% margins
- No multiple re-rating for 3+ years

This scenario is plausible if competitive dynamics in MENA streaming worsen faster than expected.

## Methodology Cross-Reference

### Why Two Methods Converged at SAR 48–50

The DCF and SOTP arriving at essentially the same answer (SAR 48.8 vs. SAR 49.4) is significant but not definitive. Both methods share a common assumption: **Shahid's streaming business is valued on its growth trajectory, not current profitability.** The DCF embeds this in terminal value; the SOTP embeds it in the EV/Revenue multiple.

Where they diverge is in the risk scenario:

- **DCF** is most sensitive to WACC (discount rate). At 12% WACC, value drops to SAR 37.7.
- **SOTP** is most sensitive to Shahid's multiple (3x vs. 8x EV/Revenue creates a SAR 25,000M swing in equity value).

The convergence is meaningful but should be treated as a **central estimate with wide confidence intervals** rather than a precise target.

## Conclusion

MBC Group at SAR 26.26 is a **speculative BUY** for investors who:

1. Believe in the MENA streaming story (Shahid as the Arabic Netflix)
2. Are patient (12–24 month re-rating horizon)
3. Understand the risks (streaming competition, advertising cycles)

The stock offers a **60–98% upside** to our fair value range of SAR 42–52, with a **central target of SAR 47**. Two independent valuation methods — DCF and SOTP — arrive at nearly identical conclusions. The primary risk is not the fundamentals; it is the timing and competitive dynamics of streaming in MENA.

The investment is not without risk. The bear case (SAR 20–25) is plausible if streaming economics disappoint. But the current market price treats Shahid as worthless — a pricing we believe overstates the risk given MBC's dominant position in Arabic-language media and the structural tailwinds of Vision 2030.
