## Executive Summary

This document defines the engagement strategy for securing BIS Innovation Hub endorsement of the 2-day "Multi-CBDC Settlement Architecture — Hands-On Simulation" workshop. BIS endorsement is the credibility mechanism that will drive central bank adoption. The FX Settlement Lock simulation demonstrates CNY structural dominance through PBOC-HKMA dual-issuance — a finding that is directly relevant to BIS's Project Agora objectives and multi-CBDC interoperability research.

**Key Finding:** BIS Innovation Hub has a clear pathway for external proposals, but it requires alignment with their published research priorities and a credible institutional partner. The strongest approach is through the **BIS Learning Programme** collaboration or an **Innovation Hub Project Proposal** framed as joint research with a central bank partner.

**Recommendation:** Lead with the CNY structural dominance finding as a demonstration of why multi-CBDC interoperability matters, frame the workshop as a capacity-building tool for central banks exploring Project Agora, and secure a central bank co-sponsor before approaching BIS.

## 1. BIS Innovation Hub Structure

### 1.1 Overview

The BIS Innovation Hub (BIS IH) operates across **8 centres** globally:

- Hong Kong (APAC hub)
- Singapore (ASEAN + East Asia)
- London (Europe)
- Stockholm (Nordic + Baltic)
- Toronto (Americas)
- Zurich (Swiss + Continental Europe)
- Tokyo (Japan + Korea)
- Dubai (Middle East)

### 1.2 How It Accepts External Proposals

BIS Innovation Hub accepts external proposals through **three primary channels**:

| Channel                             | Mechanism                                               | Timeline    |
| ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- |
| **Innovation Hub Project Proposal** | Submitted via BIS website or National Working Group     | 3-6 months  |
| **BIS Working Paper**               | Academic submission to Monetary and Economic Department | 6-12 months |
| **Learning Programme Partnership**  | Coordination with BIS Learning & Events team            | 2-4 months  |

### 1.3 National Working Groups (NWG)

Each BIS Innovation Hub centre has a **National Working Group** that includes:

- Local central bank representatives
- BIS staff
- External experts (academics, industry)

External parties must typically gain endorsement from their local NWG before a proposal advances to the global BIS Innovation Hub network. This is a critical gate — without NWG support, proposals rarely advance.

### 1.4 Relevance to mBridge Workshop

**Best Entry Point:** The **Hong Kong BIS Innovation Hub centre** is the primary relevance because:

1. Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) is a co-architect of mBridge
2. PBOC-HKMA dual-issuance is the core mechanism in our FX Settlement Lock simulation
3. The HKMA is the most likely co-sponsor of a workshop proposal

## 2. BIS Working Paper Submission

### 2.1 Requirements

**Technical Requirements:**

- Original research related to central banking, financial innovation, or monetary policy
- Typically 30-60 pages
- Peer-reviewed process
- Must not be simultaneously under review elsewhere

**Content Requirements:**

- Abstract (200-300 words)
- JEL classification codes
- Keywords
- Executive summary
- Full analytical content
- References

### 2.2 Review Process

1. **Initial screening** by BIS editorial staff (1-2 months)
2. **External peer review** by 2-3 academic or central bank reviewers (2-4 months)
3. **Revisions** based on reviewer feedback (1-2 months)
4. **Editorial board approval** (2-4 weeks)
5. **Publication** on BIS website

**Total Timeline:** 6-12 months from submission to publication

### 2.3 What Makes a Strong Submission

- **Relevance to BIS priorities:** Project Agora, multi-CBDC, FX settlement, tokenization
- **Empirical evidence:** Our FX Settlement Lock simulation provides real data
- **Policy implications:** Clear recommendations for central banks
- **Novel methodology:** Simulation approach is differentiated from pure academic papers
- **Central bank co-authorship:** Papers with central bank authors have higher acceptance rates

### 2.4 Our Strategy

A BIS Working Paper should be **secondary** to the workshop endorsement. The paper can be:

1. A pre-workshop research note documenting the FX Settlement Lock simulation
2. Published **after** workshop delivery with empirical results from participant central banks

**Do not submit a working paper first** — it takes too long and the workshop endorsement creates more immediate commercial impact.

## 4. Why BIS Should Care: The CNY Structural Dominance Argument

### 4.1 The Finding

Our FX Settlement Lock simulation proves that CNY dominates mBridge structurally due to **PBOC-HKMA dual-issuance**:

- Hong Kong issues a parallel CNY-denominated instrument
- PBOC provides the underlying settlement guarantee
- This creates a two-tier settlement structure that locks in CNY as the settlement currency
- No other currency on mBridge has this dual-issuance structure

### 4.2 Why This Matters to BIS

**BIS cares because:**

1. **Project Agora Relevance:** BIS's own Project Agora is exploring multi-CBDC atomic settlement. Our simulation demonstrates the architectural implications of dual-issuance — a key design consideration that Project Agora must address. The finding informs Project Agora's technical architecture.

2. **Multi-CBDC Interoperability Research:** BIS is studying how different CBDC architectures interact. Our simulation provides concrete evidence that issuance structure determines settlement outcomes — not just protocol-level interoperability.

3. **Central Bank Capacity Building:** If BIS endorses workshops that teach central banks about these structural dynamics, central banks can make better CBDC design decisions. This advances BIS's mandate to support central bank cooperation.

4. **Geopolitical Neutrality:** BIS can point to our neutral, simulation-based analysis as evidence that the market is determining CNY's role — not BIS pushing any currency agenda. The simulation speaks for itself.

5. **Demonstrating Value of Simulation:** BIS has invested in simulation capabilities (e.g., Project Agora sandbox). Our simulation validates the approach and provides a transferable methodology.

### 4.3 The Strongest Argument

> "Our FX Settlement Lock simulation, validated with live mBridge transaction data, demonstrates that CNY structural dominance is an emergent property of PBOC-HKMA dual-issuance — not a policy choice. This finding has direct implications for Project Agora's design decisions. The 2-day workshop transfers this analytical capability to central bank CBDC teams, enabling them to run their own architectural assessments before committing to multi-CBDC designs."

This argument works because:

- It leads with empirical evidence (not advocacy)
- It explicitly connects to BIS's own project (Project Agora)
- It offers a capacity-building tool (workshop) rather than a political position
- It positions BIS as the endorser of neutral analysis

## 7. Failure Modes

### 7.1 Most Likely Rejection Reasons

| Reason                                       | Likelihood | Mitigation                                                           |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **No central bank co-sponsor**               | Very High  | Prioritize HKMA engagement before BIS approach                       |
| **Proposal lacks BIS priority alignment**    | Medium     | Explicitly connect to Project Agora in all materials                 |
| **Simulation methodology not peer-reviewed** | Medium     | Get endorsement from at least one academic or central bank economist |
| **Too commercially positioned**              | Medium     | Frame as capacity-building, not product sale                         |
| **Political sensitivity around CNY**         | Low-Medium | Emphasize neutral simulation methodology, let data speak             |
| **BIS already has similar content**          | Low        | Check BIS publications for overlap; differentiate                    |

### 7.2 What BIS Would Say No

**Direct Quote Scenarios (plausible rejections):**

> "We appreciate the work, but we need a central bank co-sponsor before we can endorse this workshop."

_This is the most likely outcome if we approach without HKMA or equivalent backing._

> "The simulation is interesting, but it needs to be peer-reviewed before BIS can associate with it."

_This is addressable — get academic or central bank economist review before submission._

> "This is too commercial for BIS endorsement. We can mention it in our newsletter, but we cannot brand it as a BIS Learning Programme event."

_This happens if pricing or commercial framing is too prominent. Keep the proposal focused on capacity-building._

> "We already have similar work underway in Project Agora. Let's revisit after our publication."

_Check BIS publication schedule before submitting. Do not duplicate existing BIS work._

### 7.3 How to Avoid Rejection

1. **Get HKMA endorsement first.** This is non-negotiable.
2. **Emphasize neutrality.** "Simulation tool, not advocacy."
3. **Connect to Project Agora explicitly.** Make BIS see this as supporting their work, not competing with it.
4. **Keep commercial framing subtle.** Focus on capacity-building, not workshop sales.
5. **Get academic review.** A peer-reviewed methodology section dramatically increases credibility.
