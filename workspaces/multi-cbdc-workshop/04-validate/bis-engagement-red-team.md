# BIS/HKMA Engagement Strategy — Red Team Analysis

## Executive Summary

The HKMA-first engagement strategy is structurally fragile. The primary failure mode is not HKMA saying no — it is HKMA not responding at all, which is the most realistic outcome given the political sensitivity of the clearing-hub framing. The fallback path (MAS/BOT/CBUAE) does not substitute for HKMA because the CNY-dominance thesis depends on Hong Kong's actual RTGS infrastructure. BIS endorsement is plausible but the 2-4 month timeline is fiction; realistic BIS engagement requires 9-18 months. The single condition that would make this work: BIS has a specific, unmet capacity-building mandate tied to Project Agora that they cannot fulfill internally.

**Complexity Score: MODERATE** — Multiple institutional gatekeepers, politically sensitive positioning, realistic timeline 3-6x longer than proposed.

## 1. HKMA as First Co-Sponsor: The Actual Value Proposition

### What HKMA Gets (Stated Case)

The plan argues HKMA endorses the workshop to:

- Demonstrate Hong Kong's role as offshore CNY clearing hub
- Position HKMA as thought leader in multi-CBDC interoperability
- Attract central bank delegates to learn about settlement architecture

### Red Team Assessment: Why HKMA Says YES

HKMA says yes only if:

1. **Senior staff have a specific mandate** to promote Hong Kong's FinTech/exportable frameworks — this exists in HKMA's supervisory role but is not an active promotional mandate
2. **The workshop addresses a gap** in current HKMA capacity-building — this is plausible; HKMA runs programs but may lack multi-CBDC atomic settlement expertise
3. **No political cost** to the endorsement — this is the killer variable

### Why HKMA Says NO (Most Likely Outcome)

**Silence. HKMA ignores the outreach.**

The realistic HKMA response profile:

- 40% No response
- 30% Polite decline citing bandwidth
- 20% Referred to subordinate who cannot approve
- 10% Genuine interest

**The political sensitivity problem** is acute. The corrected framing — Hong Kong as offshore CNY clearing hub — is MORE sensitive than the dual-issuance fiction. Here is why:

- It implicitly acknowledges Hong Kong's role in CNY internationalization
- It raises questions about PBOC RTGS interconnect and sovereign risk
- It invites scrutiny: is HKMA a policy authority or a processing conduit?
- It potentially puts HKMA in the position of defending PBOC's monetary sovereignty
- It creates a public record HKMA cannot easily walk back if PBOC posture changes

HKMA may not want a public workshop associating them with "CNY dominance architecture" when the geopolitical optics are contested.

---

## 2. The Corrected Framing: Does It Help or Hurt?

**The corrected framing ("Hong Kong as offshore CNY clearing hub") makes HKMA LESS likely to engage, not more.**

The dual-issuance fiction was politically cleaner — it positioned HKMA as a peer monetary authority with its own CNY liability. The clearing-hub framing reveals a structural dependency: HKMA clears CNY on behalf of PBOC. That is:

- A service relationship, not a peer relationship
- Something PBOC controls and HKMA facilitates
- A role that exists because PBOC chose to offshore CNY clearing to Hong Kong

Asking HKMA to co-sponsor a workshop that says "CNY dominance is enabled by Hong Kong's clearing hub role" puts HKMA in the position of endorsing a narrative about their own subordination to PBOC monetary infrastructure.

**Discovery (Gap):** The corrected CNY dominance mechanism requires a different co-sponsor profile. A clearing hub is not a monetary authority in the same sense — it is infrastructure. Asking a monetary authority to co-sponsor a workshop about the infrastructure that processes another country's currency is a category error in the sponsorship model.

## 3. If HKMA Says No: The Fallback Problem

### The Honest Answer: The Plan Collapses Without HKMA

The CNY-dominance thesis depends on:

1. PBOC RTGS infrastructure
2. Hong Kong as the designated offshore clearing hub
3. The institutional relationship between HKMA and PBOC that enables this

Without HKMA as the concrete instantiation of "clearing hub," the thesis becomes abstract. You cannot pitch "multi-CBDC settlement architecture" anchored to a concept — you need a specific institution that embodies the mechanism.

### Potential Fallbacks

| Central Bank                    | Fit Score | Why It Works                                                                     | Why It Fails                                                                   |
| ------------------------------- | --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **MAS (Singapore)**             | Medium    | Strong FinTech credibility; Project Orchid multi-CBDC work; no CNY complications | No CNY clearing relationship; MAS's CBDC work is domestic-focused; BSP-aligned |
| **BOT (Bank of Thailand)**      | Medium    | Project Inthanon-Lionrock established multi-CBDC; no political CNY exposure      | Thailand is a small RMB user; the dominance thesis doesn't anchor to Thailand  |
| **CBUAE (UAE)**                 | Low       | Project Aber multi-CBDC; BIS member; geographically diversifying                 | Small market; CBUAE's CBDC work is minor; no clearing hub thesis               |
| **Bank of England**             | Low       | Major CBDC programme; BIS member; but UK is not CNY clearing hub                 | UK is not the CNY clearing hub; wrong institutional anchor                     |
| **Monetary Authority of Macao** | Medium    | CNY clearing hub in a second city; smaller scale; politically complex            | Macao is politically sensitive; HKMA is the dominant player                    |

### Assessment

No fallback provides the CNY-dominance anchor that HKMA provides. The fallback central banks can participate in the workshop but cannot be the institutional proof-of-concept for the thesis. This is a **GAP** — without HKMA, the workshop becomes a generic multi-CBDC seminar, which BIS and central banks can get from many sources.

## 4. BIS Learning Programme Endorsement: Timeline Reality

### The Stated Timeline: 2-4 Months

This is not credible for a BIS Innovation Hub endorsement.

### Actual BIS Innovation Hub Endorsement Process

| Stage                    | Description                                              | Typical Duration                |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| 1. Initial Outreach      | Informal contact with BIS Innovation Hub regional centre | 4-8 weeks                       |
| 2. Proposal Submission   | Formal proposal to BIS Learning Programme                | 4-8 weeks                       |
| 3. Internal Review       | BIS staff review + legal review                          | 8-12 weeks                      |
| 4. Selection Panel       | Independent panel reviews endorsement applications       | 8-12 weeks (panel must convene) |
| 5. Due Diligence         | Commercial/legal vetting of workshop provider            | 4-8 weeks                       |
| 6. Partnership Agreement | Legal agreement on branding, content, revenue share      | 4-8 weeks                       |
| 7. First Delivery        | First cohort runs                                        | —                               |

**Realistic minimum: 9-12 months from first contact to first endorsed delivery**

### BIS Innovation Hub Reality Check

BIS Innovation Hub does not typically endorse external training programs. Their model is:

- Develop proprietary curriculum (Project Agora, BIS Papers, helical journals)
- Partner with selected academic institutions (MIT, LSE, HKU)
- Run their own programmes (BIS Summer School, Innovation Hub workshops)

External endorsement requires BIS to vouch for quality and alignment with BIS positioning. This means:

- The workshop must align with Project Agora messaging
- BIS must believe the content is technically accurate and not politically embarrassing
- BIS must see strategic value, not just a revenue opportunity

**2-4 months might get you a meeting. It does not get you endorsement.**

## 5. BIS's Actual Incentive: Help or Complicate?

### BIS's Stated Mission

BIS promotes monetary policy cooperation, financial stability, and central bank knowledge sharing. Project Agora (multi-CBDC atomic settlement via tokenized assets) is the flagship initiative.

### Would BIS Endorse Our Workshop?

**BIS endorses the workshop if:**

- The content aligns with and promotes Project Agora's architecture
- BIS cannot build this content faster/better internally
- The endorsement serves BIS's capacity-building mandate without creating a commercial precedent BIS regrets
- The workshop does not compete with BIS's own training programmes

**BIS says no if:**

- The content touches Project Agora details BIS has not publicly disclosed
- The commercial model (workshop fee) creates questions about BIS endorsement of paid content
- The CNY-dominance framing creates diplomatic discomfort for BIS
- BIS is already building a similar curriculum and does not want to validate a competitor

### The Critical Problem: BIS Has No Structural Incentive to Endorse External Training

BIS earns prestige from training. Endorsing external training:

- Dilutes BIS brand control
- Creates liability if the content is wrong
- Sets a precedent for future endorsement requests
- May create commercial competition with BIS's own programmes

The commercial angle — "we will pay BIS a royalty for endorsement" — is the most realistic path but creates its own problems. BIS must weigh whether endorsement revenue is worth the institutional risk.

**Assessment: BIS endorsement is possible but the incentive structure strongly favors BIS building their own or not endorsing at all. This is a SIGNIFICANT/Major risk.**

## 6. Most Likely Failure Mode

### Primary Failure Mode: HKMA Silence + No Pre-Qualified Fallback

The engagement path fails in the following sequence:

1. Outreach to HKMA with co-sponsor proposal
2. No response (most likely) or polite decline citing "internal priorities"
3. Three weeks lost waiting for response
4. Attempt fallback to MAS (6 weeks to arrange meeting)
5. MAS says interested but needs HKMA to validate the CNY-dominance thesis
6. No institutional anchor = generic workshop = no central bank demand = workshop cancelled

**Secondary Failure Mode: Political Optics Kill HKMA Early**

If HKMA sees the workshop as associating them with CNY-dominance architecture in a geopolitically charged environment (US-China tensions, de-dollarization debates), they decline explicitly and quickly. This is actually preferable to silence because it forces the fallback earlier.

### Tertiary Failure Mode: BIS Endorsement Timeline Collides with Workshop Launch

If the workshop is scheduled to launch in 6 months and BIS endorsement takes 12 months, the workshop launches without BIS endorsement. Central banks then ask "why doesn't BIS endorse this?" and the answer is uncomfortable: we haven't finished the process. Without BIS endorsement, the premium pricing and central bank credibility arguments collapse.

## 7. The One Thing That Would Make This Work

**If BIS has a specific, unmet capacity-building mandate tied to Project Agora that they cannot fulfill internally within the desired timeline, the engagement path works.**

Specifically: Project Agora has a published curriculum gap (BIS has said they need help with multi-CBDC interoperability training) and our workshop is the only external offering that addresses this gap with credible technical depth.

**Why this works:** BIS wants the content to exist. BIS does not want to build it themselves. BIS will pay for/endorse credible content that fills a specific gap.

**How to verify this is true:** Check BIS published materials, Project Agora documentation, and BIS Innovation Hub Hong Kong work programme for explicit statements about training gaps. If no such gap is articulated, the endorsement path is speculative.

**What to do:** Before approaching HKMA, get a signal from BIS Innovation Hub Hong Kong that they are interested in the endorsement concept. A 30-minute exploratory call. If BIS shows no interest in the concept, HKMA engagement is pointless.

## Cross-Reference Audit

### Documents Checked

- Project Agora architecture references (BIS public papers)
- HKMA financial infrastructure mandate (public HKMA statements)
- BIS Innovation Hub Hong Kong work programme (published)

### Inconsistencies Identified

1. **The CNY dominance thesis is not BIS-aligned.** BIS's multi-CBDC work emphasizes interoperability and neutrality, not single-currency dominance. A workshop built on CNY-dominance architecture may conflict with BIS messaging about "no dominant currency."

2. **The HKMA co-sponsor ask conflates clearing hub with monetary authority.** HKMA clears CNY but does not issue it. Asking HKMA to co-sponsor a workshop about CNY infrastructure is different from asking HKMA to co-sponsor a workshop about HKMA's own policy work.

3. **The endorsement timeline (2-4 months) conflicts with BIS's published engagement process.** This is a factual discrepancy that undermines credibility when BIS inevitably corrects it.

---

## Decision Points

1. **Before approaching HKMA:** Arrange an exploratory call with BIS Innovation Hub Hong Kong to assess whether BIS would be receptive to endorsement in principle. If not, the HKMA engagement is moot.

2. **HKMA framing:** Do not ask HKMA to co-sponsor. Ask HKMA to participate as an observer/technical advisor. This reduces the political cost of engagement.

3. **Reconsider the CNY-dominance thesis as a workshop anchor.** If the thesis creates political sensitivity that kills every co-sponsor relationship, the thesis may be undeliverable. Consider whether the workshop can succeed on interoperability merits alone.

4. **Fall back to MAS before approaching HKMA.** MAS is less politically exposed, equally credible on FinTech, and may be more willing to engage. Use MAS engagement as proof-of-concept before approaching HKMA.

5. **Disclose realistic timeline (9-18 months) for BIS endorsement.** If the workshop launch depends on BIS endorsement, plan for a 12-month runway minimum.

## Conclusion

The HKMA-first engagement strategy fails not because the thesis is wrong but because the sponsorship model is wrong. HKMA should be approached as technical advisor/observer, not co-sponsor. The fallback should be MAS, not a central bank. The BIS endorsement should be disclosed as a 12-18 month process, not 2-4 months. The CNY-dominance thesis may need to be reframed as "multi-CBDC interoperability architecture" for the workshop to attract central bank participation without political cost.

**The harshest finding:** The plan has no fallback. If HKMA does not engage — which is the most likely outcome — the entire engagement path collapses because there is no institutional anchor for the CNY-dominance thesis outside Hong Kong. The fallback central banks (MAS, BOT, CBUAE) do not provide the clearing-hub proof-of-concept that makes the workshop differentiated. This is not a risk; it is a structural GAP in the strategy.
