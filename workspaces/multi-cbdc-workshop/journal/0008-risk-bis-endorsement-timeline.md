## Finding

The stated 2-4 month timeline for BIS Learning Programme endorsement is not credible. Based on BIS Innovation Hub published engagement processes and organizational structure, a realistic endorsement timeline is 9-18 months from first contact to first endorsed delivery.

### Actual BIS Endorsement Process Stages

| Stage                 | Description                                              | Duration                        |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Initial Outreach      | Informal contact with BIS Innovation Hub regional centre | 4-8 weeks                       |
| Proposal Submission   | Formal proposal to BIS Learning Programme                | 4-8 weeks                       |
| Internal Review       | BIS staff review + legal review                          | 8-12 weeks                      |
| Selection Panel       | Independent panel reviews endorsement applications       | 8-12 weeks (panel must convene) |
| Due Diligence         | Commercial/legal vetting of workshop provider            | 4-8 weeks                       |
| Partnership Agreement | Legal agreement on branding, content, revenue share      | 4-8 weeks                       |

**Realistic minimum: 9-12 months from first contact to first endorsed delivery**

## Why This Matters

If the workshop launch depends on BIS endorsement, the current launch plan is structurally undeliverable. The gap between stated timeline (2-4 months) and realistic timeline (9-18 months) means:

1. Workshop launches without BIS endorsement
2. Central banks ask why BIS does not endorse
3. Premium pricing and institutional credibility arguments collapse
4. Workshop competes as generic offering rather than BIS-endorsed programme

## Mitigation

- Disclose realistic 12-18 month timeline upfront
- Stage engagement: exploratory BIS call BEFORE approaching HKMA
- Plan for BIS-endorsed second cohort, not first cohort
- Consider launching without BIS endorsement and treating endorsement as a future milestone
