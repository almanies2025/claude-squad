## Finding

A workshop anchored to CNY-dominance architecture may conflict with BIS institutional messaging about multi-CBDC interoperability. BIS's Project Agora emphasizes neutrality, interoperability, and no single-currency dominance. A workshop that frames CNY as architecturally dominant may create diplomatic discomfort for BIS and be inconsistent with BIS's published positioning on the future of international payments.

### BIS Published Multi-CBDC Messaging

BIS and BIS Innovation Hub publications on multi-CBDC interoperability consistently emphasize:

- "No single currency should dominate" — neutrality across CBDC designs
- Interoperability as a public good
- Avoidance of single-currency hegemony in CBDC design
- Preservation of monetary policy sovereignty

A workshop that claims CNY has architectural dominance in the future payment system — even if technically accurate about current RTGS flows — conflicts with the institutional narrative BIS promotes.

### Why This Matters for Endorsement

BIS endorsement requires:

1. Technical accuracy (the content must be correct)
2. Strategic alignment (the content must serve BIS messaging goals)
3. No diplomatic discomfort (the content must not embarrass BIS with member central banks)

A CNY-dominance workshop fails on dimension 2 and potentially dimension 3. BIS cannot endorse a workshop that contradicts its own stated principles of multi-currency neutrality without creating a public record that undermines its diplomatic position.

### The Inconsistency

| BIS Stance                      | Workshop Thesis                   |
| ------------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Neutrality across CBDCs         | CNY has architectural dominance   |
| No single-currency hegemony     | CNY-dominance is the core thesis  |
| Interoperability as public good | Interoperability serves CNY flows |

## Implication

If the workshop cannot drop the CNY-dominance framing, BIS endorsement becomes structurally unavailable regardless of timeline. The workshop could still be delivered commercially, but without BIS endorsement it cannot achieve the premium institutional positioning required for the business model.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Reframe the workshop thesis** as "Multi-CBDC Interoperability Architecture" with CNY as one case study, not the dominant architecture
2. **Lead with interoperability, not dominance.** The technical content can include CNY-dominance findings without making it the organizing principle
3. **Check BIS Project Agora public materials** for explicit language that conflicts with CNY-dominance framing before investing in the endorsement path
4. **Accept trade-off:** The reframe may reduce the workshop's differentiation but makes BIS endorsement achievable
