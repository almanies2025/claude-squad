## Discovery

The SDK's genuine analytical merit (FX Settlement Lock as graph connectivity problem) has immediate value in a different market: **treasury technology consulting and research tooling**.

**Viable short-term path**:

- Target: Treasury technology advisors, CBDC research firms, central bank consultancies
- Engagement model: Project-based consulting ($25K-50K per engagement)
- Market size: $500K-1M annually (10-20 engagements)
- Timeline: Can begin in 2026, no vendor procurement required

**Why this works**:

- Consulting clients buy methodology, not software modules
- No integration burden, no vendor approval required
- CBDC researchers need topology analysis tools today
- Builds relationships that may yield vendor access later

**Contrast with TMS vendor path**:

- TMS vendor licensing: $75K-150K annual license × uncertain uptake = ~$200-400K potential revenue in 3 years
- Consulting path: $500K-1M annually, available now, no regulatory dependency

**The pivot is not a compromise**: It deploys the same analytical capability through a go-to-market model that matches the current market reality.

## For Discussion

1. **Market validation**: What is the specific universe of consulting clients? Can we name 10-15 firms or institutions that would buy CBDC topology analysis engagements today?
2. **Capacity constraint**: If consulting generates $500K-1M annually, how many concurrent engagements does the team support? Is there a bottleneck on analytical capacity?
3. **Strategic value**: Does consulting work build toward the vendor path (relationships, credibility) or replace it entirely? Are these mutually exclusive or complementary?
