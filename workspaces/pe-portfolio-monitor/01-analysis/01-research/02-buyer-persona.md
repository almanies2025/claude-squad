# Buyer Persona — PE Portfolio Monitoring

## Who Actually Buys

Three plausible buyers; only one is the economic buyer at the mid-market firm size.

## Primary Economic Buyer: The Operating Partner

At a $500M–$5B AUM firm with 10–20 portcos, the operating partner is typically:

- 40s–50s, ex-consultant (Bain/McKinsey/BCG Ops) or ex-portco CEO/CFO
- 1–3 operating partners per firm; each covers 4–8 portcos
- Compensation tied to portco performance (carry on specific deals they own)
- **Owns the "something is going wrong at PortCo X" problem** — exactly what this product solves

**Day-to-day pain:**

- Drinking from a firehose of portco data in inconsistent formats
- Finding out about problems at the monthly board meeting, not when they started 6 weeks earlier
- Has to personally call the portco CFO to understand a number — which is political friction
- Spends 30–40% of time on data gathering vs. value creation
- Board-pack preparation consumes a full week each quarter across the portfolio

**Purchase trigger:** A specific portco where they missed a deteriorating signal and the board/IC asked "why didn't we see this sooner?" This is a fresh-wound moment.

**90-day success:**

- All 10–20 portcos connected
- First real anomaly surfaced before the portco's monthly close
- At least one avoided-loss or accelerated-intervention story to tell GPs at quarterly partner meeting
- Board pack prep time cut from 5 days to 1 day

## Secondary Buyer: CFO of the PE Firm

- Runs fund accounting, LP reporting, valuations
- Cares about auditable data lineage, SOC 2, LP-reporting automation
- **Usually a blocker, not a champion** — concerned about data quality and regulatory exposure
- Is the buyer if the product is pitched as "LP-reporting automation + monitoring on top"

## Tertiary: The Managing Partner / Founding GP

- Signs the check above $100K
- Does not live in the tool; will see a monthly summary
- Champion only if the operating partner has already endorsed

## Decision-Making Dynamics

| Role                         | Motion                              | Typical Posture                                 |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Operating Partner            | **Champion**                        | Actively needs this; will drive internal buy-in |
| Head of Portfolio Operations | **Champion or co-champion**         | Same pain                                       |
| CFO / Head of Finance        | **Blocker-then-enabler**            | Satisfied by SOC 2 + data quality story         |
| IT / Head of Technology      | **Technical gatekeeper**            | SOC 2, data residency, integration review       |
| Managing Partner             | **Approver**                        | Signs at $75K+ ACV; wants outcome narrative     |
| Portco CFO                   | **External stakeholder — can veto** | Must grant ERP access                           |

## The Hidden Gatekeeper: The Portco CFO

The product only works if 10–20 portco CFOs grant ERP access. Each of those CFOs:

- Reports to the PE firm but is not employed by them
- Has legitimate concerns: data security, audit trail, what happens in an acrimonious exit
- Has the political power to slow-walk the request for months

**Implication:** The product must have a portco-CFO-friendly narrative. "We're replacing the monthly data request you hate" is the pitch; "we're surveilling your finances" is the anti-pitch.

## Purchase Triggers (Ranked)

1. **Fresh wound** — a specific portco loss/write-down the firm missed (~50% of deals)
2. **Fund-cycle milestone** — new fund close, institutionalizing for Fund IV/V LPs (~20%)
3. **New operating partner hired** — they demand better tooling (~15%)
4. **LP pressure** — institutional LPs asking for more granular reporting (~10%)
5. **Proactive modernization** — rare (<5%)

**Triggers 1 and 3 are the best ICP signals for outbound targeting.**
