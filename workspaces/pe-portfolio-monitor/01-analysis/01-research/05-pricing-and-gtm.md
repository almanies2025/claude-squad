## Recommended Pricing Structure

**Target ACV at mid-market ($500M–$5B AUM, 10–20 portcos): $60K–$120K annually**

**Structure: Platform base fee + per-portco connector fee**

| Component                                                        | Price           |
| ---------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| Platform base (dashboard, anomaly engine, LP reporting, 3 users) | $30K/yr         |
| Per-portco ERP connector                                         | $3K–$5K/yr each |
| Additional user seats (above 3)                                  | $2K/yr each     |

**Worked example** — $1B AUM firm, 15 portcos, 5 users:

- Base: $30K
- 15 connectors × $4K = $60K
- 2 extra seats × $2K = $4K
- **Total ACV: $94K**

## Why Per-Portco, Not Per-AUM

- Per-AUM doesn't scale cost to work — a $2B fund with 10 portcos costs less to serve than a $1B fund with 25 portcos
- Per-portco aligns cost-to-deliver with revenue (each connector has ongoing maintenance cost)
- Enables a "start with 3 portcos, expand" land motion rather than all-or-nothing commitment

## GTM Motion

### Year 1: Founder/Champion-Led (No BDR)

The target buyer population is small (~500 firms in ICP). Traditional BDR-driven funnels don't work.

1. **Founder-led sales** via operating-partner network — every OP knows 5–10 others
2. **Design partner model** — first 3–5 customers at 30–50% discount for references and co-development
3. **Conference circuit** — ACG InterGrowth, SuperReturn North America, ILPA events, Operating Partners' Forum, Kayo Operators' Summit
4. **Content / thought leadership** — "what we see across 50 mid-market portcos" as inbound driver

**Year 1 target**: 8–12 customers at ~$60K ACV = $500K–$700K ARR

### Year 2–3: Scaling

- 1–2 AEs with PE-tech backgrounds (ex-operating partner, ex-Bain, ex-iLEVEL)
- Marketing hire for thought leadership + events
- Targeted outbound to the ~500-firm ICP universe

**Year 2 target**: $2.5M–$4M ARR (30–50 customers, $75K avg ACV)
**Year 3 target**: $8M–$12M ARR (80–120 customers, $85K avg)

## Sales Cycle: 4–7 Months

| Stage                                   | Timing     |
| --------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Discovery call with operating partner   | Week 0–2   |
| Demo with OP + Head of Finance          | Week 3–5   |
| Technical review (IT/security, SOC 2)   | Week 6–10  |
| Paid pilot with 2–3 portcos ($15K–$25K) | Week 10–18 |
| Commercial negotiation + legal          | Week 18–22 |
| Signed contract                         | Week 22–30 |

**Paid pilots** ($15K–$25K, 8 weeks) filter tire-kickers and are standard in this category.

## Sales Narrative: Lead with Operating Partner, Close with CFO

- **Headline in first meeting**: live ERP + multi-signal anomaly detection (operating-partner pain)
- **Deal-closer when CFO is in the room**: LP-reporting automation (CFO validation)

Don't lead with LP reporting — it positions the product as a commodity.

## Unit Economics

| Metric                       | Target                                                        |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| CAC                          | $40K–$60K per customer                                        |
| Payback period               | ~9–12 months at $60K ACV                                      |
| Gross margin                 | 70–80% (lower than pure SaaS due to ERP maintenance overhead) |
| Net revenue retention target | 110%+ (portfolio expansion drives connector + seat growth)    |

## GTM Risks

| Risk                                         | Mitigation                                                  |
| -------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Category-creator fatigue ("another PE tool") | Lead with sharp operating-partner wedge                     |
| Long sales cycles burn cash                  | Paid pilots; deposit-on-signature                           |
| Portco CFO veto at pilot stage               | Portco-facing value narrative + SOC 2 collateral            |
| Reference-starvation in Y1                   | Over-invest in design-partner success; accept low margin Y1 |
| 73 Strings ships competing feature mid-cycle | Ship faster; be specific about the wedge                    |
