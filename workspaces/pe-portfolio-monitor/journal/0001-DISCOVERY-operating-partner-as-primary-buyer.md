# Operating Partner as Primary Buyer

The economic buyer for a PE portfolio monitoring product is the **operating partner** (or head of portfolio operations / head of value creation), not the firm's CFO.

This is counter-intuitive because the product involves financial data and the CFO is the obvious owner of financial tools. But the operative pain belongs to the operating partner:

- They are compensated on portco performance (carry tied to specific deals)
- They own the "something is going wrong at PortCo X" problem
- They are the ones who get asked "why didn't we see this sooner?" when a portco deteriorates
- They spend 30–40% of their time gathering data rather than creating value

The CFO is typically a **blocker-then-enabler**: concerned about data quality, regulatory exposure, and audit trails — satisfied by SOC 2 collateral and accurate numbers, then becomes the deployment owner.

**The hidden gatekeeper is the portco CFO.** They must grant ERP access but don't work for the PE firm. They have legitimate concerns (surveillance, data security, exit acrimony) and can slow-walk access for months. The product needs a portco-CFO-friendly narrative: "we're replacing the monthly data request you hate," not "we're surveilling your finances."

## Why This Matters

- GTM motion: founder-led via operating partner network, not CFO network
- Sales narrative: anomaly detection and time-to-signal (OP pain) as headline; LP reporting automation (CFO pain) as deal-closer when CFO enters the room
- Product design: PE Partner heatmap as primary surface; portco CFO view designed to build trust, not demonstrate capability
- Churn risk: if the founding operating-partner champion leaves the firm, account is at risk unless the product is embedded enough in the firm's workflow

## For Discussion

1. The operating partner's compensation is tied to specific deals — their incentive is to catch problems in _their_ portcos, not the whole portfolio. Does the product need per-deal owner assignment as a first-class concept, or is portfolio-wide visibility sufficient for the OP to value the subscription?

2. If the portco CFO veto is the top deployment risk, what is the minimum portco-facing value exchange that makes CFOs _want_ to connect rather than resist? Peer benchmarks? Free cash-flow forecasting? The answer significantly shapes product roadmap.

3. If three firms in a close-knit PE network (e.g., all Boston-based mid-market) each adopt the product, does that create a network effect where portco CFOs are more willing to connect because they see peers doing it — or does it create a concern about data concentration across the industry?
