## Executive Summary

The US payment account float market represents **$50-80B in annual interest income** that banks earn on consumer and business demand deposits. FloatYield's B2B infrastructure opportunity lies in enabling fintechs and neobanks to capture a portion of this float as yield for their customers, taking a platform fee per account. The TAM is substantial ($50-80B), but the SAM via B2B platform sales is likely $500M-2B in Year 3, with SOM in the $50-200M range given sales cycles and bank relationships.

**Key insight:** The stablecoin market (USDC/USDT at ~$150B) is the most direct competitor for yield-bearing payment accounts and is capturing float that traditional banks ignore. This represents both the clearest market validation and the most imminent competitive threat.

## 1. US Payment Account Market Size

### Consumer Transaction Accounts

- **~180 million** US adults have checking accounts (FDIC data)
- Average checking account balance: **$3,000-8,000** per account
- Total consumer checking deposits at US banks: **~$2.5-3T**

### Business Transaction Accounts

- **~30 million** US businesses have checking accounts
- Average business checking balance: **$10,000-50,000** per account
- Total business demand deposits: **~$3-4T**

### Total Market

| Segment           | Accounts  | Total Balance | Annual Float Income (at 4%) |
| ----------------- | --------- | ------------- | --------------------------- |
| Consumer Checking | 180M      | $2.5-3T       | $100-120B                   |
| Business Checking | 30M       | $3-4T         | $120-160B                   |
| **Total**         | **~210M** | **$5.5-7T**   | **$220-280B**               |

_Note: Float income to banks is the gross interest earned. Banks pay ~0.1-0.5% to customers, capturing the spread._

### Float Economics

- **Average neobank balance per user:** ~$500-1,500
- **Total neobank user balances:** ~$25-50B
- **Current yield offered:** None (neobanks compete on UX, not yield)
- **Interest paid to users:** Essentially zero
- **Float capture opportunity:** If neobanks offered 3% yield on $30B in user deposits, customers would earn ~$900M/year; banks currently capture this entire spread

**Key finding:** Neobanks have the user base but lack the regulatory infrastructure and banking relationships to offer yield. This is FloatYield's primary go-to-market channel.

**Key finding:** Brex and Ramp are already offering yield. This validates market demand but means FloatYield must compete on infrastructure provision, not first-mover advantage.

## 4. TAM/SAM/SOM Calculation

- Consumer checking float income: **$100-120B/year**
- Business checking float income: **$120-160B/year**
- **Total TAM: $220-280B/year**

_Assumptions: 4% Fed funds rate, ~$5.5-7T in total balances_

### SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)

**Float income accessible through B2B platform sales:**

- Target: Neobanks, fintechs, community banks, credit unions
- Assume 50 neobank partners with 50M total users
- Average balance: $1,000 = $50B in floatable balances

_Conservative SAM: $500M-2B/year in platform fees_

_Conservative SOM: $100-200M ARR by Year 3_

## 5. Stablecoin Market — Direct Competition

### Market Size

| Stablecoin    | Market Cap    | Annual Yield Generated |
| ------------- | ------------- | ---------------------- |
| USDT (Tether) | $110-130B     | $4.4-5.2B (at 4%)      |
| USDC (Circle) | $45-55B       | $1.8-2.2B (at 4%)      |
| **Total**     | **$155-185B** | **$6.2-7.4B**          |

### Yield Distribution

- **USDT holders:** Capture 3-5% yield (via Treasury backing)
- **Yield earned by holders:** ~$6-8B annually
- **Competition:** This yield is available NOW, without banking relationship

### Why Stablecoins Are the Real Competitor

1. **No banking required** — users self-custody
2. **Yield guaranteed** — 3-5% on Treasury reserves
3. **No FDIC insurance** — but users may not care
4. **Growing adoption** — USDC used by major DeFi protocols

**Risk:** If stablecoins achieve mainstream UX parity, they could capture the entire consumer yield market. Circle and Tether are already winning.

**Opportunity:** FloatYield's B2B partners can offer yield with FDIC insurance (via partner banks), which stablecoins cannot provide.

## 6. Marcus by Goldman Sachs — Direct Incumbent

### Product Overview

- **Launch:** October 2016
- **AUM:** ~$100B+ (as of 2024)
- **Yield:** 4.0-4.2% APY (competitive with high-yield savings)
- **Target:** Consumer mass affluent ($25K+ in savings)

### How Marcus Works

1. Goldman Sachs takes deposits
2. Invests in Treasury securities and mortgages
3. Pays 4%+ APY to consumers
4. Earns spread on Fed funds vs 4%+ yield

### Competitive Implications

- **Consumer direct:** Marcus captures the mass affluent consumer directly
- **B2B threat:** Marcus could launch B2B infrastructure for fintechs
- **Pricing pressure:** Marcus sets the consumer yield ceiling at ~4.2%

**Key insight:** Marcus proves the float economics work at scale. FloatYield's B2B model is differentiated by enabling fintechs to offer their own branded yield accounts rather than competing directly.

## 9. Key Findings and Recommendations

### Single Biggest Market Insight

**The stablecoin market is the real competition, not traditional banks.** USDC and USDT at $150B+ market cap are capturing yield that neobanks and fintechs ignore. This represents both a $6-8B annual market being missed by traditional players and the clearest validation that consumers want yield on payment balances.

### Market Opportunity Size

- **TAM:** $220-280B (annual float income in US banking system)
- **SAM:** $500M-2B (B2B platform revenue potential)
- **SOM:** $100-300M (Year 3 conservative estimate)

### Recommended Go-to-Market

1. **Phase 1:** Partner with neobanks (Dave, Current, Cleo) who lack yield products
2. **Phase 2:** Expand to community banks and credit unions
3. **Phase 3:** Pursue enterprise payment processors

### Critical Success Factors

1. **Speed:** Marcus and stablecoins are moving fast
2. **Banking partnerships:** Need 2-3 partner banks for FDIC insurance
3. **Regulatory clarity:** Section 26A and state money transmitter rules must be navigated
4. **Unit economics:** Must demonstrate clear ROI vs. neobanks holding zero-yield deposits
