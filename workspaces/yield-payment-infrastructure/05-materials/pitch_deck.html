The Arithmetic

$5.5–7T in checking deposits generates $220–280B/year in float income at 4%. Banks pay customers 0.1–0.5%. The spread is pure profit.

The Validation

Stablecoins (USDC + USDT, $150B+ AUM) prove customers will shift for yield. They earn issuers ~$6–7B/year in seigniorage.

Neobanks with 20M+ users offer 0% yield. They have the users — but not the regulatory path.

The Solution

B2B infrastructure that lets banks and fintechs offer interest-bearing payment accounts — without their own bank charter.

Business Model Redesign Red Team Fixes

Fix #1: Tiered bps on ADB

Original problem: Revenue scales with accounts; liability scales with deposits. At Year 3: 30–75% of revenue eaten by reconciliation losses.

Solution: 75→65→50 bps on Average Daily Balance. Revenue now scales with float. 5.6× reconciliation coverage.

Fix #2: CUSO Structure

Original problem: Sponsor bank bears FDIC risk; FloatYield captures upside. At scale, bank has incentive to build alternatives.

Solution: Partner with credit unions (NCUA-insured). Aligned incentives — they need loanable deposits.

Red Team: Key Risks

Critical Risks

  • ● Reconciliation divergence: ~$900K/yr on $1B book
  • ● CUSO partnership takes >18 months (vs. 6-12 projected)
  • ● ILC charter denied (~50% denial rate 2024-2026)
  • ● Sponsor Bank terminates at Year 2–3

Fatal Risk: Cash Flow Timing

ILC needs $4–8M capital. Capital raised from projected CUSO fees. But CUSO takes 12–18 months. Fee revenue delayed → runway depleted → ILC application lapses → model collapses.

Classic fintech death spiral.

Interest Rate Risk

Below 3.5% Treasury, banks don't need FloatYield. Current 4.5% is optimal. Build fee-based revenue diversification.

Mitigations

  • Raise capital upfront (not from future fees)
  • Buffer CUSO timeline by 6 months
  • 3-year min Sponsor Bank contract
  • ILC denial contingency planned
