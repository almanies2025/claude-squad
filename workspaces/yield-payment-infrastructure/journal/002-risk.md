# RISK: Sponsor Bank Termination Triggered at Year 3 Scale

## Risk

The sponsor bank conflict is not just a renegotiation risk — it is an existential threat that activates at Year 3 scale.

**Scenario**: At Year 3, FloatYield generates $7.5M/year in revenue on $1.5B in deposits. The sponsor bank:

- Bears FDIC exposure on $1.5B
- Earns ~$45–90M/year in interest spread
- Carries compliance and regulatory burden
- May conclude the arrangement no longer justifies the risk

**The bank can terminate with 90–180 days notice**, forcing FloatYield to either:

1. Accept worse terms (bank captures more margin)
2. Emergency migration to CUSO or ILC at Year 3 scale (high risk)

**Bank incentive to renegotiate**: At $7.5M FloatYield revenue, the bank has strong basis to claim "we built this market" and demand 30–40% platform economics.

## Mitigation

1. **CUSO migration before Year 3**: Complete 2–3 credit union partnerships before hitting 500M ADB
2. **ILC charter as strategic objective**: 24–36 month runway to full independence
3. **Contractual protection**: In current agreement, negotiate 3-year minimum term with automatic renewal unless either party elects not to renew — this limits termination frequency

## Severity

High. If CUSO migration takes longer than 18 months, FloatYield enters Year 3 with significant termination exposure and no alternative in production.

## Status

Open — mitigation requires immediate action (CUSO partnership outreach in parallel with fee model rollout)
