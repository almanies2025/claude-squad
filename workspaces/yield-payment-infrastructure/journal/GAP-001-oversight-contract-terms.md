## Gap: Program Manager Agreement Framework Does Not Address Post-Synapse Regulatory Requirements

**Severity:** MAJOR
**Category:** Legal/Compliance
**Root Cause:** The Program Manager Agreement template or framework assumed in FloatYield's planning predates the 2024-2025 regulatory guidance on BaaS oversight

### Gap Description

Post-Synapse (2024-2026), federal banking regulators expect Sponsor Banks to demonstrate "real oversight" of BaaS programs. The FFIEC's BaaS examination guidance and FDIC FIL-29-2024 require specific contractual and operational provisions that are absent from standard BaaS agreements:

**Required Provisions:**

1. **Documented Oversight Program**
   - Written BaaS oversight policy approved by bank's board
   - Named BSA/AML officer with explicit authority over the program
   - Not just "fintech handles compliance" — bank must have independent program

2. **Independent Validation Rights**
   - Bank's right to audit fintech's compliance program annually
   - Third-party validation of fintech's KYC/AML effectiveness
   - Access to transaction data and customer complaint logs without fintech filtering

3. **Veto Authority Over Product Changes**
   - Bank's compliance officer must approve new product features before launch
   - Material product changes require regulatory notification
   - Clear definition of what constitutes a "material change"

4. **Termination Triggers**
   - Specific definitions of compliance failures requiring termination
   - Automatic termination triggers (not discretionary) for certain violations
   - Regulatory notification requirements built into termination procedures

5. **Indemnification for Compliance Failures**
   - FloatYield must indemnify the bank for regulatory actions caused by FloatYield's compliance failures
   - Errors and omissions insurance requirements
   - Capital maintenance requirements for FloatYield

### Why This Matters

If FloatYield signs a standard Program Manager Agreement without these provisions, the bank will fail its next regulatory examination. When the OCC or FDIC tells the bank to clean up its BaaS program, the first thing they require is contract amendments that FloatYield may not have negotiated — putting FloatYield in a weak position to resist unfavorable terms.

### Mitigation

1. **Banking counsel review:** Before signing any agreement, have experienced BaaS banking counsel review and negotiate terms
2. **Reference 2025-2026 guidance:** Ensure the agreement explicitly references current regulatory expectations
3. **Audit rights are non-negotiable:** If the bank won't agree to annual audits, the partnership is not viable under current regulatory expectations
4. **Oversight appendices:** Require detailed oversight appendices that document the actual oversight program, not just general terms
