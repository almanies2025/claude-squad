## Gap: 3-6 Month Launch Timeline Does Not Account for Post-Synapse Reality

**Severity:** MAJOR
**Category:** Planning/Strategic
**Root Cause:** FloatYield's timeline estimates were likely derived from pre-Synapse BaaS benchmarks that are no longer valid

### Gap Description

FloatYield's planning assumes a 3-6 month timeline from partnership signing to live program. This timeline is based on pre-Synapse BaaS benchmarks. Post-Synapse (2024-2026), the timeline has expanded significantly:

**Realistic Post-Synapse Timeline:**

| Phase                                 | Pre-Synapse | Post-Synapse                                                          |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Initial outreach to bank              | 2-4 weeks   | 4-8 weeks (banks are more selective, due diligence longer)            |
| Letter of Intent / Term Sheet         | 4-8 weeks   | 8-16 weeks (negotiations more complex due to regulatory requirements) |
| Program Manager Agreement negotiation | 4-8 weeks   | 12-24 weeks (oversight provisions require detailed negotiation)       |
| Regulatory notification/approval      | 2-4 weeks   | 4-12 weeks (regulators scrutinizing BaaS more heavily)                |
| Technical integration                 | 8-12 weeks  | 12-16 weeks                                                           |
| Compliance program build-out          | 4-8 weeks   | 8-16 weeks (banks require more robust compliance documentation)       |
| Pilot testing                         | 4-8 weeks   | 8-12 weeks                                                            |
| Full launch                           | 4-8 weeks   | 8-12 weeks                                                            |

**Total:** 9-18 months minimum, not 3-6 months.

### Why This Matters

A 3-6 month timeline assumption creates several problems:

1. **Capital planning is wrong.** If FloatYield plans for 6 months of runway to launch, it will run out of money at month 6 with no live product.

2. **Competitive positioning fails.** If FloatYield tells a bank they can be live in 6 months and the bank expects 12-18 months, trust erodes immediately.

3. **Investor expectations are wrong.** Investors who underwrite a 6-month timeline will lose confidence when the actual path is 12-18 months.

4. **Product roadmap gaps.** If the plan is to launch in 6 months, the product roadmap does not account for the 12+ month gap before meaningful revenue.

### Evidence

- Column Bank, one of the most BaaS-friendly banks, now requires 12+ months for new partner onboarding
- Synapse's collapse has caused all major BaaS banks to extend due diligence timelines
- OCC and FDIC examination cycles for BaaS programs now include more rigorous initial assessment
- Banking counsel report that Program Manager Agreement negotiations now regularly extend to 6+ months (up from 4-8 weeks pre-Synapse)

### Mitigation

1. **Revise the business plan** to reflect 12-18 month timeline to meaningful revenue
2. **Inform investors** of revised timeline and extend fundraising runway accordingly
3. **Parallel path development:** Initiate alternative regulatory path (credit union charter) in parallel to reduce dependency on single timeline
4. **Bank outreach now:** Start outreach immediately — the clock must start now if the target is a 2027 launch
